New York State — Who Governs

The elected officials governing the State of New York — Governor, Lieutenant Governor, Attorney General, Comptroller, and the State Legislature (Senate + Assembly). New York's government controls a $237B+ annual budget, affecting 19.7 million residents and setting policy for the state's 62 counties. All statewide and legislative candidates must file campaign finance disclosures with the NYS Board of Elections. The Legislature maintains a public bill tracking system (legislation.nysenate.gov).

Governor

Governor — Chief Executive Officer Kathy Hochul (D)
Kathy Hochul was sworn in as New York's 57th Governor on August 24, 2021, following Andrew Cuomo's resignation. She was elected to a full 4-year term in November 2022, defeating Republican Lee Zeldin 53%–47%. Hochul controls the Executive Budget, appoints state agency commissioners, and issues executive orders. The Governor's Executive Budget is the primary document for state fiscal policy. Campaign finance: elections.ny.gov. Official: governor.ny.gov.
Source: governor.ny.gov ↗ · NYS Board of Elections ↗

Attorney General

Attorney General — Chief Law Officer Letitia James (D)
Letitia James was elected Attorney General in 2018 (first Black woman to hold the office) and re-elected in 2022. The AG enforces state law, prosecutes public corruption, protects consumers, and oversees charitable organizations. James led the investigation that resulted in Andrew Cuomo's resignation and filed major antitrust and consumer protection actions. Official: ag.ny.gov.
Source: ag.ny.gov ↗

State Comptroller

Comptroller — Fiscal Watchdog Thomas DiNapoli (D)
Thomas DiNapoli has served as NYS Comptroller since 2007, re-elected in 2022. The Comptroller independently audits state agencies and local governments, manages the NYS Common Retirement Fund (~$250B AUM, 1.1 million public employee beneficiaries), and issues annual State of the State fiscal analyses. DiNapoli's audits are public and searchable at osc.ny.gov. Official: osc.ny.gov.
Source: osc.ny.gov ↗

State Senate (63 seats)

Senate Majority Leader Andrea Stewart-Cousins (D-Westchester)
Andrea Stewart-Cousins leads the 63-member NY State Senate (40D/23R as of 2023). The Senate must pass all state legislation and the Executive Budget. Key committee chairs control health, finance, judiciary, and housing policy. Full Senate roster and committee assignments at nysenate.gov. Campaign finance for all senators at elections.ny.gov.
Source: nysenate.gov ↗

State Assembly (150 seats)

Assembly Speaker Carl Heastie (D-Bronx)
Carl Heastie has served as Assembly Speaker since 2015. The 150-member Assembly (102D/48R as of 2023) originates appropriations bills and co-passes all state legislation. Key committees: Ways & Means (budget), Judiciary, Health, Housing. Full Assembly roster at nyassembly.gov. Campaign finance at elections.ny.gov.
Source: nyassembly.gov/mem ↗
