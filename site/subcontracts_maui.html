Who Is Really Paid Behind the Prime? sourced

When a federal award lands on Maui, the money does not stop at the prime contractor — it flows down to the subrecipients (subcontractors on contracts, subgrantees on grants) who do part of the work. USASpending.gov publishes that lower tier from the recipients' own SAM.gov / FSRS filings. This maps those chains for Maui County (place of performance FIPS 15009): which primes route the most money down, which firms surface beneath the most different primes, and the heaviest single prime→sub links — every one a question to verify against the public record, never an accusation.

Source: USASpending.gov subaward tier (spending_by_award, spending_level=subawards), place of performance = Maui County (FIPS 15009), window 2021-10-01–2026-09-30. Split: contracts $22,313,913 across 44 subcontracts · grants $148,731,001 across 257 subgrants. Every record carries a sourced provenance badge (federal SAM.gov/FSRS filing, not a meeting transcript). Being a prime or a recurring sub is lawful and ordinary — the map exists so the chain can be read, not so anyone is accused. Firm names fold case/suffix variants ("ECC CONSTRUCTORS LLC" = "Ecc Constructors, L.L.C."). Coverage: 301 subawards join 159 distinct primes to 80 subrecipients. For context, 800+ Maui prime awards were scanned separately — the prime tier and the subaward tier are each recorded against place of performance independently, so they are two lenses on the same county, not a subset of one another.
Named gap — the county (non-federal) sub tier is not public. This page is the federal subcontractor chain only. A Maui County vendor's own subcontractors on a county-funded job are not published in any open dataset: Hawaiʻi has no equivalent of New York City's Local Law 44 subcontractor-disclosure feed. Until the County discloses development-team / subcontractor rosters (or a records request obtains them), the county sub tier is a known blank — stated here, not invented.
