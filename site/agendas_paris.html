Agendas — Paris

Know what is being decided before it is decided. Upcoming public meetings as far ahead as this government posts them — so the community can show up, testify, and participate in time.

Source: Ville de Paris — the Conseil de Paris "ordre du jour" (order of the day) web application, the official system where the Mayor of Paris publishes session agendas, deliberation projects, and downloadable PDF documents. Reached via paris.fr (Municipalité > Conseil de Paris > Ordre du jour). ↗  source verified
Horizon: Rolling, tied to the next plenary session. Three agenda versions are posted per session: a provisional list of deliberation projects on the Tuesday 12 days before the session, an updated agenda the Tuesday before, and the definitive version the Friday before. The Conseil de Paris meets roughly 4-10 times/year, so typically only the next session's agenda is published at a time. · showing: verified snapshot
