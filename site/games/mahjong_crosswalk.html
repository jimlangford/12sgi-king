54 nodes. 108 tiles. One canon.

The SAGE 54-node spine maps cleanly onto the Mahjong suit deck: 108 numbered tiles ÷ 2 = 54 unique node-tile pairs. Four SAGE lineages map to four Mahjong winds. This is a lens — explore the crosswalk between ahupuaʻa stewardship and the tile-wind tradition.

Mahjong has four wind tiles (East · South · West · North) × 4 copies + 4 honor sets. The four SAGE lineages (Mauka · Kula · Makai · Universal) map to these four cardinal directions.

The core equation

108 Mahjong suit tiles ÷ 2 copies = 54 unique suit types
54 unique suit types = 54 SAGE nodes

A standard Mahjong deck contains 136 tiles total: 108 suit tiles + 16 wind tiles + 12 dragon tiles. The suit tiles alone — Bamboo, Circles, Characters × ranks 1–9 × 2 copies = 108 suit tiles. Dividing by 2 (to count unique tile types rather than copies) gives exactly 54.

Suit distribution

Three suits × 9 ranks × 2 copies = 54 tiles per suit group = 54 unique types:

  • Bamboo: ranks 1–9 × 2 = 18 tiles → N01–N18 (Mauka spine + early Kula)
  • Circles: ranks 1–9 × 2 = 18 tiles → N19–N36 (mid Kula + Makai spine)
  • Characters: ranks 1–9 × 2 = 18 tiles → N37–N54 (late Makai + Universal)

Each of 54 SAGE nodes maps to one unique suit-rank pair. Low ranks (1–3) = Ao phase; high ranks (7–9) = Pō phase; mid (4–6) = transition.

Wind = lineage mapping

  • East wind → Mauka lineage (N01–N14) — morning, the rising sun over the upland ridge
  • South wind → Kula lineage (N15–N27) — noon heat, the mid-elevation flatlands
  • West wind → Makai lineage (N28–N41) — evening, the ocean facing west
  • North wind → Universal lineage (N42–N54) — night, the stars, spirit layer above all zones

Ao · Pō as tile pairing

Every Mahjong suit tile appears exactly twice in the full deck. This duality mirrors the SAGE Kumulipo structure: each node has an Ao (day) face — active civic choices — and a Pō (night) face — the balance the creative system resolves overnight.

Two copies of each tile = two phases of each node. When both copies are played (both phases completed), the node is whole.
