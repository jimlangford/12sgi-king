Demand the Records

The contracts most likely to hide corruption — sole-source awards made without competition — aren’t published anywhere public. Under Hawai’i’s open-records law (UIPA, HRS 92F), any person can demand them. The more people who ask, the harder they are to ignore.

⚠ Why this matters. A Maui Environmental Management director was sentenced to 10 years in federal prison for steering $19M+ in sole-source contracts to a briber (U.S. DOJ). That award log was never posted publicly. This request pries it open — lawfully. See the record →

1. Copy the request

Open the County records portal ↗ Email the Council records office

Executive-branch contracts (where the Stant awards were) go through the County portal. The email button reaches the Council’s records office (ocs.request@mauicounty.us). Paste the copied text, add your name, and send.

2. Sign up — add your name to the demand

Get notified when the records come back, and show the County how many people want them released.

⚙ Owner setup (one time, ~2 min): create a free form at formspree.io with your email, then replace YOUR_FORM_ID in this form’s action with your form ID. Until then, submissions won’t be collected.

Your info goes only to the campaign organizer to send you updates. It is not shared or sold.

Integrity: UIPA requests seek public records — facts. Asking for a contract log is not an accusation against any person; it is how the public verifies for itself.
