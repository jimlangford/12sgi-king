## §12-202-23 Enforcement (verbatim, as amended 08/25/24)
"§12-202-23 Enforcement. (a) The director shall…" — the notice of violation and order may require, among other things, that "an **initial civil fine be paid not to exceed $100,000 per violation**; that a civil fine be paid **not to exceed $10,000 per day for each** [day the violation persists]…". Section references a "**notice of warning or notice of violation**."

## §12-202-25 Penalties (verbatim, as amended 08/25/24)
"§12-202-25 Penalties. Any person who violates … shall be liable for an **initial civil fine not to exceed $100,000 per violation and maximum daily fine of $10,000** in addition to any other penalties until [corrected]. A civil fine may be imposed by the department after an opportunity for an appeal hearing under chapter 91, Hawaii Revised Statutes…" including initial and accumulating daily fines. [Eff 1/1/94; am 9/28/97; am and comp 08/25/24] (Auth: HRS §205A-32)

## SUPERSESSION NOTE (analysis label)
The 2003 CC opinion (ArchiveCenter Item 288) interpreted the THEN-current Maui SMA rules (§12-202-23(d)(1) ≤$10,000/violation + ≤$1,000/day; §12-202-25 ≤$10,000 + $1,000/day) and concluded NO warning was required. The **08/25/2024 amendment** (a) raised the tier to **$100,000 / $10,000-day** (matching Molokaʻi Ch. 302 / Lānaʻi Ch. 402), and (b) the amended §12-202-23 now references a "**notice of warning or notice of violation**." Treat the 2003 opinion as historical on both the amount and the warning point; verify current §12-202-23 text before relying.
