# SMA & Shoreline Enforcement — Warnings Before Fines (2003 Corporation Counsel Opinion)

> **LABEL: DATED LEGAL OPINION — interpretation, not black-letter law.** This is a July 2, 2003 Maui County Department of the Corporation Counsel memorandum opinion (author: Deputy Corporation Counsel Cindy Y. Young; to Planning Director Michael W. Foley). It interprets statutes/rules as of 2003 and **may be superseded** by later amendments, rules, or case law. Cited here for the enforcement corpus; verify current HRS/rules before relying.
> **Source:** Maui County ArchiveCenter, Item 288 — https://www.mauicounty.gov/ArchiveCenter/ViewFile/Item/288 (retrieved 2026-06-17 via web_fetch; full text extracted).

## Question presented
Whether the Department of Planning may issue a notice of violation and assess a fine for Special Management Area (SMA) and shoreline setback violations **without first issuing a prior written warning**. (Planning had believed three written warnings were required before a notice of violation under SMA Rules §§12-202-23 and 12-202-25 and Shoreline Rules §12-5-15.)

## Authorities quoted in the opinion (verbatim citations)
- **HRS §205A-32 (Penalties).** (a) A violator of part II or part III is liable for (1) a civil fine **not to exceed $100,000**, or (2) the cost of returning the affected coastal environment/ecology to its pre-violation condition. (b) In addition, liability for a civil fine **not to exceed $10,000 a day** for each day the violation persists. (c) Any civil fine/penalty "**may be imposed by the circuit court or may be imposed by the department after an opportunity for a hearing under chapter 91.**"
- **SMA Rules §12-202-23(d)(1).** Landowner/alleged violator notified by certified/registered mail of an alleged violation; the **notice of violation and order** states the section violated, nature, and remedies; may require the activity cease or development be removed; **a civil fine not to exceed $10,000 per violation** and **not to exceed $1,000 per day** the violation persists, in addition to other penalties.
- **SMA Rules §12-202-25 (Penalties).** Initial civil fine **≤$10,000 per violation** + maximum daily fine of **$1,000** until corrected; "**A civil fine may be imposed by the [planning] department after an opportunity for a hearing under chapter 91, HRS … unless said hearing is otherwise waived.**" A later SMA permit application does not stay an order to pay fines.
- **Shoreline Rules §12-5-15 (Penalties).** Violators "**shall be subject to the penalties provided for in Chapter 205A-32, HRS.**"
- **HRS §46-1.5(24)(A).** Each county may impose fines (in addition to criminal penalties) for violation of **county ordinances or rules** "**after reasonable notice and requests to correct or cease the violation have been made upon the violator**"; civil fine may be administratively imposed after a ch. 91 hearing. *(The CC office had interpreted this to require **at least two warnings** before fining for a county ordinance/rule violation.)*

## Analysis (as stated in the opinion)
- HRS Chapter 205A, the SMA Rules, and the Shoreline Rules **do not require written warnings** before assessing a fine.
- HRS §46-1.5(24)(A)'s warnings interpretation is **inapplicable** to enforcement under HRS Ch. 205A: §205A-32 is **independent statutory authority** to impose civil fines for parts II/III violations.
- **Due process** does not require a pre-notice warning in the 205A context; concurrent notice that a fine may be assessed is adequate. Citing **Price v. Zoning Board of Appeals of City & County of Honolulu, 77 Hawaiʻi 168, 883 P.2d 629 (1994)** (fines for a zoning-ordinance violation assessed prior to a contested-case hearing did not violate due process) and *Bruno v. D.C. Bd. of Appeals & Review*, 665 A.2d 202 (D.C. 1995).
- Note: HRS §205A-32 was amended in 2001 to add (a)(2) (cost of environmental restoration); that subsection does not require an opportunity to abate before a fine.

## Conclusion (verbatim)
"…no warning is required to be provided before the Department issues a notice of violation and assesses a civil fine under HRS Chapter 205A and the SMA Rules and Shoreline Rules."

## Why this matters for the enforcement corpus (the contrast to surface)
- **County ORDINANCE / RULE violations** (e.g., Title 19 enforced under county authority, HRS §46-1.5(24)(A)) → CC office reads the statute to expect **reasonable notice / warnings to correct before fining** (CC interpretation: ≥2 warnings).
- **SMA / shoreline violations** (HRS Ch. 205A → SMA Rules / Shoreline Rules) → **no warning required**; a notice of violation and civil fine may issue at once (subject to a ch. 91 hearing opportunity). Different, independent statutory track with much higher caps (**up to $100,000; up to $10,000/day** under §205A-32).
