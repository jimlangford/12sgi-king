## 1. Compliance required — § 19.530.010
"Any approval or permit issued pursuant to the provisions of this title shall comply with all applicable requirements of this article." (Ord. 2032 § 5 (part), 1991)

## 2. Criminal prosecution — § 19.530.020
A person convicted of a violation of Title 19 is sentenced as follows:

- **First offense:** fine not exceeding **$1,000** plus either 32 hours community service (HRS § 706-605(1)(f)) or 48 hours imprisonment. [19.530.020(A)(1)]
- **Second offense within 5 years of a prior conviction:** fine not exceeding **$1,000** plus either 64 hours community service or 96 hours imprisonment. [19.530.020(A)(2)]
- **Subsequent conviction within 5 years of two prior convictions:** fine of **not less than $500 but not exceeding $1,000** plus either 64–140 hours community service or 96 hours–30 calendar days imprisonment. [19.530.020(A)(3)]
- **Continuing violations:** after a conviction for a first violation, "each further day of violation shall constitute a separate offense" where the violation is a continuance. [19.530.020(B)]

## 3. Administrative enforcement (civil fines) — § 19.530.030
Civil enforcement is available "**In lieu of, or in addition to, enforcement by criminal prosecution.**"

**Who may enforce:** the director of public works, the director of environmental management, the director of water supply, or the planning director, where any person "is violating or has violated any provision of titles 8, 12, 14, 16, 18, 19, and 20 of this code, any rules adopted thereunder, or any permit issued thereto." The director serves a **notice of violation and order** by mail (with proof) or personal delivery; if that fails, by conspicuous posting on the property.

**Contents of the order** [19.530.030(B)(1)] — the order may require the person to:
- (a) Cease and desist from the violation;
- (b) Correct the violation at the person's own expense before a specified date;
- (c) Pay a **civil fine not to exceed $1,000** — *except the initial civil fine shall not exceed **$20,000** for operating a bed and breakfast home, short-term rental home, transient vacation rental, or other transient accommodation without a required permit, unless a higher fine is authorized by State law;*
- (d) Pay a **civil fine not to exceed $1,000 per day** for each day the violation persists — *except the daily civil fine shall not exceed **$10,000** for the same unpermitted transient-accommodation operations, unless a higher fine is authorized by State law;*
- (e) Pay a civil fine not to exceed **1 percent of the project cost** as provided in § 20.08.260(E)(2).

**Finality & appeal** [19.530.030(B)(2), (C)]: the order becomes **final unless an appeal is filed with the Board of Variances and Appeals within thirty (30) days** after the date of mailing or delivery. "However, an appeal to the board of variances and appeals shall not stay any provision of the order."

**Collection of unpaid civil fines** [19.530.030(D)]: in addition to other collection procedures, "the county may add unpaid civil fines … to any county taxes, fees or charges except for residential water or sewer charges."

**Judicial enforcement** [19.530.030(E)]: the enforcing director may institute a civil action in any court of competent jurisdiction to enforce the order.

## Enforcement process (plain-language flow — restatement of the above)
1. Director (Planning / Public Works / Environmental Management / Water Supply) determines a violation of Title 19 (or related titles / rules / permits) exists.
2. **Notice of violation and order** served by mail, personal delivery, or posting.
3. Order may require cease-and-desist, correction by a date, and/or civil fines (per-violation and/or per-day; elevated caps for unpermitted transient accommodations; or up to 1% of project cost).
4. Order becomes **final after 30 days** unless appealed to the **Board of Variances and Appeals**; appeal does **not** stay the order.
5. Unpaid fines may be attached to County taxes/fees (not residential water/sewer); director may also seek **judicial enforcement**.
6. Alternatively or additionally, **criminal prosecution** under § 19.530.020 (fines + community service/imprisonment; each day a separate offense).
