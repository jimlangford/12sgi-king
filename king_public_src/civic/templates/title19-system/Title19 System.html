Both charters, side-by-side — per Title 19 area

County (operative law, cited) Sovereign (foundation · James's analysis)
Plan authority & conformance foundation
▲ Maui County (law)Charter §8-8.5: the general plan = countywide policy plan + Maui Island Plan + community plans; all zoning & subdivision must conform to it. The Council enacts Title 19; the Planning Dept (Charter Art. 8) drafts; Planning Commissions administer.
▼ Sovereign (foundation)The whole county structure rests on the sovereign charter beneath it. Sovereign The foundation defines the purpose the operative system is meant to serve.
Agriculture & food diverge
▲ Maui County (law)Agricultural district Ch. 19.30A + Rural Ch. 19.29; uses must conform to the general plan; enforced by the Zoning Administration & Enforcement Division.
▼ Sovereign (foundation)Food Security Protect + activate working lands for local provisioning. The County's own rewrite audit says "ag zoning isn't working" — the foundation would put food production first.
Housing & community design diverge
▲ Maui County (law)Community plans (Ch. 2.80B) guide future use; zoning must conform to the general plan (§8-8.5); the Planning Dept drafts, the Council adopts.
▼ Sovereign (foundation)Sovereign Education Design communities for the people's flourishing; give the plans real force rather than letting interim/stacked zoning override them.
Process & transparency partial
▲ Maui County (law)Planning Commission(s) (Ch. 2.28) hold noticed public hearings; SMA under the Coastal Zone Management law; the Planning Director determines minor vs substantial change.
▼ Sovereign (foundation)Truth Changes that affect the public must be re-noticed and re-heard — nothing slips through as "minor." Aligns with the hearing process; diverges where "minor" skips the public.
Enforcement & civil fines diverge
▲ Maui County (law)The Zoning Administration & Enforcement Division cites violations; Title 19 sets the civil-fine schedule. fine schedule expanding
▼ Sovereign (foundation)Truth Sovereign Enforce equitably; fines should fund the remedy, not price out the small landowner.
Who pays / fees diverge
▲ Maui County (law)The applicant pays application + study fees per the Title 19 fee schedule; County staff conduct review.
▼ Sovereign (foundation)Sovereign Where the County imposes a requirement (study, upgrade, condition), the County should bear that cost — not download it onto the resident. (James's ⚖️ position.)
Maps & parcel access align
▲ Maui County (law)Designations are published via County/State GIS + the real-property (qPublic) record; the Planning Dept maintains GIS.
▼ Sovereign (foundation)Truth Education The public can know their land — free, on demand. The live parcel lookup already serves this; the layers align.
