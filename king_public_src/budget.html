Micro Grant Eligibility — 12 Stones Farm Operators

Agricultural Micro Grants: $3,000,000 — FarmBox Operator Strategy

The FY2026 Micro Grants program requires farm businesses operating on less than 12 acres (or up to 40 usable acres for livestock). FarmBox units on Hua Lani's 9.2-acre property qualify. Grants are distributed with equity preference to socially disadvantaged farmers, women, and Native Hawaiians. Operators must be Maui County residents for 1+ year. FarmBox operators placed by 12 Stones would qualify for individual grants of up to the full program amount per approved applicant. Strategy: place 6 FarmBox operators on Hua Lani by June 2026, each applying independently.

FarmBox Foods Partnership — Budget Alignment

FarmBox Foods (CEO Rusty Walker, rusty@farmboxfoods.com) provides the physical units at $33,200 base. Maui County's $1,917,000 Agriculture Operations/Infrastructure/Education grant line is the most direct co-application target. The 6 FarmBox units planned for Phase 1 represent $199,200 in hardware — well within the grant envelope when combined with IoT sensor integration ($1.2K/mo/site × 6 = $7.2K/mo) and WFCF traceability certification ($3K/farm/year × 6 = $18K/yr).

⚠ URGENT: USDA VAPG $500K — DEADLINE MARCH 31, 2026

The Value-Added Producer Grant (VAPG) deadline is in 3 days. This grant directly funds the FarmBox hydroponic deployment at Hua Lani and is explicitly required by MOU obligations. FarmBox Foods (Rusty Walker) must be listed as equipment supplier. Complete the grant application and file with USDA Rural Development immediately.
