Seeking a 501(c)(3) partner to sponsor our work

We are a Hawaiʻi mission building food security, rural development, affordable-housing recovery, education, and cultural media — grounded in aloha, kuleana, and the Kumulipo. We have the projects, the community, and the grant writing. We are looking for an aligned 501(c)(3) public charity to serve as fiscal sponsor so federal and foundation funds can flow to the work.

What we bring

  • A deep, ready grant pipeline — dozens of authored applications across USDA (farm innovation, rural development, value-added agriculture, food security), HUD/CDBG-DR (Lahaina recovery housing), FEMA-BRIC (resilience), NEA/arts, and education.
  • Food as infrastructure — local production (FarmBox, hydroponics, heirloom seed) that cuts Hawaiʻi's ~85–90% food-import dependence.
  • Radical transparency — a live public civic-accountability engine (govOS) that tracks money, votes, contracts, and federal dollars across Hawaiʻi as sourced questions, never accusations.
  • Culture-first media + education — film, music, and a learning game that carry Hawaiian language and lineage with reverence.

What we're asking

A 501(c)(3) fiscal-sponsorship partnership: your charitable status + our shovel-ready projects and grant capacity. Sponsored programs are reported with full transparency — every dollar mapped to a result, the same standard as our public dashboards.
