Honolulu — who lobbies and pays the deciders

Entities that appear in both public records at once: registered to lobby the State of Hawaiʻi (Ethics Commission) and a campaign donor to Honolulu candidates (Campaign Spending Commission). Lobbying is lawful; donating is lawful. Doing both is a double channel — two ways the same interest reaches the same decider — so it belongs on the map, as a question.

The question. When the same named entity both funds a candidate's campaign and pays to lobby the laws and budgets that office controls, two influence channels converge on one decider. The record below shows which entities do both, and the size of the donation channel. Read it beside their bills, their votes, and their disclosures — that is where the question gets answered.
