2026 Maui Candidates — Who's Running, and Who Funds Them

Everyone who filed nomination papers by the June 2, 2026 deadline for Mayor and all nine County Council seats — set beside the campaign money on file for each. Primary Aug 8; general Nov 3. The two-candidate Council races skip the primary and go straight to November.

Money is the reported total from the Hawaii Campaign Spending Commission record; "RE" is the real-estate / development slice. Names are matched carefully — where a filer shares a surname with a different person, their money is NOT counted here.

IMPORTANT — how to read "no money on file": Hawaii candidates must register a committee within 10 days of filing papers or once they cross $100 raised or spent, then file periodic reports on a set schedule through the Aug 8 primary and Nov 3 general. This record holds the reports filed so far. A brand-new challenger who filed in June 2026 has no prior history and their first 2026 report may not be posted yet — so "no money on file" most often means their report isn't due/posted, NOT that they raised nothing. These figures fill in after each reporting deadline. Money is lawful; the point is simply to show it beside each name, as a question.

The pattern, plainly: the money sits with the incumbents and one established challenger (Kelly King, $171K). Most challengers — and every grassroots name — start near zero; Bobby Pahia is running for an open seat on $3,000. The seats where real-estate money is heaviest (Uʻu-Hodgins 28%, the Mayor's race) are the ones to watch as the land-and-housing votes come up.
