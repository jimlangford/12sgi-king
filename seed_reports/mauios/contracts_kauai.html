For Oʻahu and Maui we can show every Notice of Award beside who funds the deciders, because those counties file their awards into the State’s open record. Kauaʻi does not. The County of Kauaʻi appears in the statewide award-notice system (HANDS) exactly once — while it runs its own procurement on two platforms that, today, a public reader cannot fetch as data. This page names the exact sources to open, and the one award that is in the open record, so the gap is documented rather than guessed.

One line is not a county’s procurement. A county that pays for roads, parks, transit buses, water systems and professional services posts far more than a single award a year — they are simply posted somewhere other than the State’s open feed. Below is where they actually live, and why each is not yet fetchable as data.

Where Kauaʻi actually posts awards — the sources to open

1 · County of Kauaʻi, Division of Purchasing — “Tabulations and Awards”
https://www.kauai.gov/Government/Departments-Agencies/Finance/Division-of-Purchasing/Tabulations-and-Awards
This is the canonical county source. By Hawaiʻi law (HRS 103D) bid tabulations and notices of award are posted here. Status: the kauai.gov host is behind Akamai edge bot-protection and returns HTTP 403 to automated fetches; awards are published as individual PDF documents per solicitation, not as an HTML table or downloadable dataset. Opening this = a PDF-harvest pass (manual or browser-driven) that extracts vendor + amount from each award PDF.
2 · Public Purchase — County of Kauaʻi procurement portal (GEMS)
https://www.publicpurchase.com/gems/kauaicounty,hi/buyer/public/publicInfo
The county runs its sealed-bid solicitations through Public Purchase. Status: the public view shows only open solicitations (e.g. IFB-G/S #4105, transit buses, closing Jun 2026), the page deliberately interleaves obfuscation spans to defeat scraping, and award results / vendor names / dollar amounts are gated behind a registered-vendor login. No closed-award dollar data is publicly fetchable from this surface.
3 · State of Hawaiʻi — HANDS award-notice feed (cross-check)
https://hands.ehawaii.gov/hands/awards
The statewide one-stop record. Status: reachable and already parsed (it is the spine of the Honolulu and Maui contract pages). Kauaʻi simply does not file into it — hence the single row above. This is the cross-reference, not the primary fix.
4 · County of Kauaʻi Open Data Hub (ArcGIS) — checked, no contracts dataset
https://kauai-open-data-kauaigis.hub.arcgis.com/
The county does run an open-data hub — but it is GIS only (parcels, flood & tsunami zones, parks, bus routes, water-quality advisories). It carries no procurement, contract-award, or vendor-payment dataset. Worth a standing watch in case a spending layer is added.

The honest read

Does Kauaʻi County’s spending answer the public the way Oʻahu’s and Maui’s now can? Today that question can’t be answered in data — not because the money isn’t there, but because the award record sits in PDFs behind a bot-wall and a vendor login. The next step is concrete and named: harvest the award PDFs at source #1 (kauai.gov Tabulations and Awards) into vendor + amount rows, then set them beside the Kauaʻi campaign-finance ledger exactly as the Maui parity page does. Until that pass runs, this page stands as the marker of the gap, not a filled-in ledger. No vendor, amount, or connection on this page is invented.
