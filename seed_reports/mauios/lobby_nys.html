Beyond donating and contracting, there is a third channel of influence: paid lobbying. Compensation and reimbursement fields are stored as text and were cast to number in code. The raw table fans every filing out across its itemized-expense rows (66.8M rows for 51,154 real filings), so each amount was taken once per filing (max per form_submission_id) before summing per beneficial client — otherwise totals inflate by orders of magnitude. Lobbying is lawful and registered by design; this maps who spends the most to influence State government, to read beside who funds officials and who wins contracts. Names matched on normalized organization across two public datasets: COELIG lobbying clients (qym9-xzj6) × NYSBOE organizational campaign donors (4j2b-6a2j). A name match is not proof of the same legal entity — common names collide, so generic tokens are stoplisted and every line is a question. Matched against 5,000 organizational donor names.
