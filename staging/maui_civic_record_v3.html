Why the map looks like this — and why that’s part of the audit

The gaps below aren’t ours to paper over, and they aren’t the council members’ to answer for. They are the county’s own publishing choices — and those choices are themselves on the public record. Naming them, with their source, is the audit doing its job.

  • No committee roll-call is published. On the Bill 9 matters the industry testified on, no recorded vote — ayes and noes by member — appears in what the county makes public, so we can’t yet color a seat. source: Maui County CivicClerk proceedings · roll-call not published (rollcall_published = 0)
  • Contracts aren’t tagged to a district. The county publishes its $118.3M in awards centrally under “Maui Director of Finance,” not by council area — so we never claim “money in District N.” source: Maui County HANDS award notices
  • The roster doesn’t bind seats to districts. The county’s official roster lists all nine members without a district number or boundary; only five home areas are named in the record, so only those five are marked. source: mauicounty.us council roster · district field empty

Each of these is a records request we’ve filed to close it. The gap is the county’s to fix — and the record of it is public.

Audit flaw #1

The one record we can’t yet reach

We can see the money. We can see who testified. But the recorded committee vote on Bill 9 — the exact thing that would tell you who each seat sided with — Maui County doesn’t publish in readable form. That’s not the council members’ doing; it’s a gap in what the county makes public. We’ve prepared a records request (HRS §92F / UIPA) to the Maui County Clerk, Council Services, for the roll-call — ayes and noes, by member — on Bill 9 and the appropriations that move the money. It’s ready to send in your own name.

Until that record arrives, no seat on this page is colored. Naming what we can’t yet see is the most honest thing an audit can do — and closing that gap is the work.

How to read a seat

Served the people, or served the money?

When all three legs are in hand, each seat takes one honest color. Today none of them do — because the vote is hidden. This is the lens, not a verdict on anyone.

Served the people

The recorded vote matched what the district testified for. Celebrated as loudly as the other is questioned.

Served the money

The vote tracked the money and not the testimony — a question worth a closer look, never an accusation.

Record gap

One or more legs can’t be sourced yet. Where we are today for all nine seats — because the roll-call isn’t published.

Current state: all nine seats — record gap. Not because nothing happened, but because the vote that would settle it isn’t in the public record yet. The moment the roll-call comes back, the seats that matched their district’s will go green, and any that tracked the money instead show gold — sourced, or they stay uncolored.
