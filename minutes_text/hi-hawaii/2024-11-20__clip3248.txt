Res. 693-24: 
(Draft 2) 
 
APPROVES THE LEGISLATIVE PROPOSALS AND PRIORITIES FOR 
INCLUSION IN THE 2025 HAWAIʻI STATE ASSOCIATION OF COUNTIES 
LEGISLATIVE PACKAGE  
Includes proposals relating to the regulation of tobacco products, deferred 
retirement for Police, the environment, cesspools, general excise tax, conveyance 
tax, and housing; and nine policy priorities relating to:  (1) increasing funds for 
emergency preparedness; (2) lowering the cost and expanding the availability of 
insurance for homeowners and businesses; (3) workforce development; (4) game 
management of wild ungulates; (5) promoting producer responsibility for solid 
waste; (6) prioritizing and promoting safe routes to school; (7) increasing food 
security and access to locally-produced food; (8) improving public safety, 
expanding and access to mental health resources, and establishing community 
courts; and (9) preventing and combatting squatters and supporting property owners 
and communities impacted by squatters.

Bill 217: 
 
AMENDS CHAPTER 6, ARTICLE 2, SECTION 6-12, OF THE HAWAIʻI 
COUNTY CODE 1983 (2016 EDITION, AS AMENDED), RELATING TO 
PROHIBITED HIGHWAYS FOR COMMERCIAL BICYCLE TOURS  
Seeks to add a portion of Kohala Mountain Road and a portion of Akoni Pule 
Highway as prohibited highways for commercial bicycle tours. 
 
Reference: Comm. 1093 
Intr. by: Ms. Evans 
First Reading: November 8, 2024 
 
 
 
Bill 223: 
 
AMENDS ORDINANCE NO. 24-32, AS AMENDED, THE OPERATING 
BUDGET FOR THE COUNTY OF HAWAIʻI FOR THE FISCAL YEAR 
ENDING JUNE 30, 2025  
Increases revenues in the Federal Grants – High Intensity Impact Grant account 
($193,277.96); and appropriates the same to the High Intensity Impact Grant 
account, for a total appropriation of $668,277.96.  These additional funds would be 
used by the Hawai‘i Police Department for overtime, travel, training, services, and 
equipment needed for operations to investigate drug-related cases. 
 
Reference: Comm. 1135
