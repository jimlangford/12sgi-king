receipt, will be made a part of the permanent record.  Please submit separate testimony for each item.

ORDER OF RESOLUTIONS 
 
Res. 779-20: ADOPTS THE COUNTY OF HAWAIʻI STREET DESIGN MANUAL TO GUIDE THE 
IMPLEMENTATION OF COMPLETE STREETS AND MULTIMODAL DESIGN 
PRINCIPLES FOR HAWAIʻI COUNTY ROADWAY PROJECTS  
Urges the Planning Director, Public Works Director, and Mass Transit Agency 
Administrator to update policies, rules, and ordinances in accordance with the County of 
Hawai‘i Street Design Manual to ensure consistency with Complete Streets principles. 
 
    Reference: Comm. 1154 
    Intr. by: Ms. Lee Loy 
 
 
BILLS FOR ORDINANCES 
 
Bill 217: AMENDS CHAPTER 24, ARTICLE 10, DIVISION 4, SECTION 24-278 OF THE 
HAWAI‘I COUNTY CODE 1983 (2016 EDITION, AS AMENDED), RELATING TO 
PEDESTRIANS  
Amends Section 24-278, Schedule 26 (Roads closed to pedestrian traffic), Subsection (a), 
Hāmākua, by adding a portion of Waipiʻo Valley Road running from the Waipiʻo Valley 
lookout to a point near its entry upon the valley floor. 
 
    Reference: Comm. 1130 
    Intr. by: Ms. Poindexter 
    Postponed: November 5, 2020 
 
((Note:  There is a motion by Ms. Poindexter, seconded by Mr. Kanealiʻi-Kleinfelder 
,to recommend passage of Bill 217 on first reading.

Submitting Video Testimony:  Those submitting video testimony may email a complete web address 
(url) to videotestimony@hawaiicounty.gov before 12:00 noon on the business day prior to the 
meeting.  The email shall indicate the appropriate Committee or Council meeting, the meeting date, 
agenda item (communication, bill, resolution, or report number), and number of testifiers on the video 
submittal.  Each video submittal shall be limited to a single agenda item.  Video submittals may 
contain up to three individual testifiers and shall each be up to three minutes in length.  Video 
testimony will not be played during the meeting; however, it will be distributed to Council Members 
prior to the meeting.  The Office of the County Clerk reserves the right to refuse testimony containing 
inappropriate content or that is not in compliance with Council Rule 13 (Public Statements and 
Testimony).
