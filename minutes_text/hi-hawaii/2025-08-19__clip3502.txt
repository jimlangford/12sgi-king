ORDER OF RESOLUTIONS 
 
Res. 262-25: AUTHORIZES THE MAYOR TO ENTER INTO AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL 
AGREEMENT WITH THE STATE OF HAWAI‘I, DEPARTMENT OF HAWAIIAN 
HOME LANDS, FOR THE OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE OF PARK 
FACILITIES LOCATED ON LAND IDENTIFIED BY TAX MAP KEY  
NO. 2-2-059:011, ALSO KNOWN AS PANAʻEWA PARK  
Allows the County to continue operating and maintaining parks and recreation facilities  
at Panaʻewa Park. 
 
 Reference: Comm. 421 
 Intr. by: Council Member Kagiwada (B/R) 
 
 
Res. 263-25: AUTHORIZES THE MAYOR TO ENTER INTO AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL 
AGREEMENT WITH THE STATE OF HAWAI‘I, DEPARTMENT OF HAWAIIAN 
HOME LANDS, TO COLLABORATE TO PROVIDE CONTROLLED ACCESS 
THROUGH LAND IDENTIFIED BY TAX MAP KEY NOS. 2-1-013:005 AND 006, 
ALSO KNOWN AS LEHIA BEACH PARK  
Allows the County and DHHL to collaborate in providing safe and secure access for the 
public and other community members who utilize the beach park property. 
 
 Reference: Comm. 422 
 Intr. by: Council Member Kagiwada (B/R)

BILLS FOR ORDINANCES 
 
Bill 68: INITIATES AN AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE VI OF THE CHARTER OF THE  
(Draft 2) COUNTY OF HAWAI‘I (2024 EDITION), RELATING TO THE ESTABLISHMENT 
OF A DEPARTMENT OF BUILDING        
Establishes a new Department of Building with the authority to regulate building and 
construction-related activities; creates a Director of Building position appointed by the 
Mayor and confirmed by the Council; and establishes a Building Codes Commission to 
advise the Mayor, Council, and Director on building and construction-related matters.  
 
   Reference: Comm. 376.3 
   Intr. by:  Council Members Kierkiewicz and Hustace 
 Postponed:       July 22 and August 5, 2025 
 
(Note:  There is a motion by Council Member Kierkiewicz, seconded by Council 
Member Hustace, to recommend passage of Bill 68 on first reading.)

Bill 72:   INITIATES AN AMENDMENT TO ARTICLES VI, CHAPTER 10, SECTION 6-10.3, 
OF THE CHARTER OF THE COUNTY OF HAWAI‘I (2024 EDITION), RELATING 
TO THE DIRECTOR OF ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT  
Requires the Director of Environmental Management to have experience in public 
administration, private business, or a related field; removes the requirement that the 
director needs to have an engineering degree or another degree in a related field. 
 
  Reference: Comm. 400 
  Intr. by: Council Member Kierkiewicz 
Postponed:      August 5, 2025 
 
(Note:  There is a motion by Council Member Kierkiewicz, seconded by Council  
 Member Inaba, to recommend passage of Bill 72 on first reading.)
