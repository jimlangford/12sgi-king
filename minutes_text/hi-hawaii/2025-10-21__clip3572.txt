ORDER OF RESOLUTIONS 
 
Res. 345-25: AUTHORIZES THE MAYOR TO ENTER INTO AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL  
AGREEMENT WITH THE STATE OF HAWAIʻI, DEPARTMENT OF LAND AND 
NATURAL RESOURCES, DIVISION OF FORESTRY AND WILDLIFE, FOR  
MUTUAL AID AND PUBLIC SAFETY COORDINATION  
Authorizes the Hawaiʻi Fire Department to enter into a mutual aid agreement with  
the Department of Land and Natural Resources, Division of Forestry and Wildlife, to 
establish clear working protocols for resource sharing, unified command structures,  
and coordinated responses in the event of wildfires that exceed either department’s 
individual capacity. 
 
 Reference: Comm. 547 
 Intr. by: Council Member Kagiwada (B/R)

BILLS FOR ORDINANCES 
 
Bill 68: INITIATES AN AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE VI OF THE CHARTER OF THE  
(Draft 3) COUNTY OF HAWAI‘I (2024 EDITION), RELATING TO THE ESTABLISHMENT 
OF A DEPARTMENT OF BUILDING        
Establishes a new Department of Building with the authority to regulate building and 
construction-related activities; creates a Director of Building position appointed by the 
Mayor and confirmed by the Council; and establishes a Building Codes Commission to 
advise the Mayor, Council, and Director on building and construction-related matters.  
 
   Reference: Comm. 376.6 
   Intr. by:  Council Members Kierkiewicz and Hustace 
 Postponed:       July 22, August 5 and 19,  
  and October 7, 2025 
 
(Note:  There is a motion by Council Member Kierkiewicz, seconded by Council 
Member Hustace, to recommend passage of Bill 68, as amended, on first reading.) 
 
Bill 89: AMENDS CHAPTER 7, ARTICLE 1, OF THE HAWAI‘I COUNTY CODE 1983  
  (2016 EDITION, AS AMENDED), RELATING TO THE APPOINTMENT OF THE  
  CIVIL DEFENSE ADMINISTRATOR        
Adds a new section requiring the Civil Defense Administrator have a minimum of  
three years of responsible administrative experience in emergency management, 
disaster planning, homeland security, or public safety services management.  In the 
event of a vacancy, the Mayor may convene a selection advisory panel consisting of  
the Fire Chief and Chief of Police to evaluate qualified applicants. 
 
   Reference: Comm. 514 
 Intr. by:  Council Member Kierkiewicz  
 
Bill 90: INITIATES AN AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE XIII, SECTION 13-8, OF THE 
CHARTER OF THE COUNTY OF HAWAI‘I (2024 EDITION), RELATING TO 
TERM OF OFFICE AND APPOINTMENT OF DEPARTMENT HEADS   
Repeals the requirement that the Mayor appoint department heads within 30 days of 
taking office, and within 60 days of any vacancy occurring during the Mayor’s term of 
office. 
 
   Reference: Comm. 515 
   Intr. by:  Council Member Onishi 
 Postponed:       October 7, 2025  
 
(Note:  There is a motion by Council Member Onishi, seconded by Council  
Member Galimba, to recommend passage of Bill 90 on first reading.)
