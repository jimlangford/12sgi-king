Bill 125: 
(Draft 5) 
AMENDS CHAPTER 6 OF THE HAWAIʻI COUNTY CODE 1983 
(2016 EDITION, AS AMENDED), BY ADDING A NEW ARTICLE RELATING 
TO COMMERCIAL BICYCLE TOURS  
Establishes annual registration requirements for any person or business operating a 
commercial bicycle tour and that the County Council may prohibit the operation of 
commercial bicycle tours on designated portions of public highways if 
transportation and public safety officials determine that operating the commercial 
bicycle tour on the designated portion of a public highway poses a threat to public 
safety. 
 
Reference: Comm. 679.90 
Intr. by: Ms. Evans 
Postponed: March 20, May 1, and May 15, 2024

Bill 138: 
(Draft 3) 
AMENDS CHAPTER 2, ARTICLE 7, OF THE HAWAI‘I COUNTY CODE 1983 
(2016 EDITION, AS AMENDED), RELATING TO PLANNING  
Requires the Planning Director to submit semi-annual reports to the County Council 
indicating those properties for which: (1) an initial time extension for the 
performance of conditions on the change of zone has been granted; (2) applications 
have been approved or denied for zoning variances, subdivision variances, 
subdivisions, ‘ohana dwelling unit permits, nonsignificant zoning changes, approval 
of plans for telecommunication antennas, or additional farm dwelling agreements; 
and (3) any application, except for registration of a short-term vacation rental, has 
been approved or denied under the sole authority of the planning director or by 
operation of applicable law when the planning director failed to approve or deny the 
application. 
 
Reference: Comm. 760.10 
Intr. by: Ms. Evans and Mr. Inaba 
First Reading: June 5, 2024 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Bill 145: 
(Draft 3) 
INITIATES AN AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE VI, CHAPTER 7, 
SECTIONS 6-7.3 AND 6-7.4; AND ARTICLE XVI OF THE HAWAI‘I COUNTY

Bill 153: 
 
AMENDS CHAPTER 4, ARTICLE 4, SECTION 4-4-26, OF THE HAWAI‘I 
COUNTY CODE 1983 (2016 EDITION, AS AMENDED), RELATING TO 
ANIMALS  
Increases the penalties for a noisy dog violation from $25 to $50 for the first 
violation, $75 to $100 for the second violation, $100 to $200 for the third violation, 
and $200 to $400 for any subsequent violation. 
 
Reference: Comm. 823 
Intr. by: Ms. Galimba

Postponed: June 5, 2024 
 
(Note:  There is a motion by Mr. Inaba, seconded by Ms. Lee Loy, to pass 
Bill 153 on second and final reading.)
