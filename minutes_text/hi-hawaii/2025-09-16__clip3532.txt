ORDER OF RESOLUTIONS 
 
Res. 303-25: AUTHORIZES THE MAYOR TO ENTER INTO AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL  
AGREEMENT WITH THE STATE OF HAWAI‘I, THE COUNTY OF KAUAʻI, THE 
COUNTY OF MAUI, AND THE CITY AND COUNTY OF HONOLULU FOR THE 
STATEWIDE MULTI-JURISDICTIONAL DRUG TASK FORCE  
Authorizes the Hawaiʻi Police Department to enter into a Memorandum of 
Understanding to interdict drugs via apprehension and arrest of individuals smuggling 
and distributing illicit narcotics into the State.   
 
 Reference: Comm. 483 
 Intr. by: Council Member Kagiwada (B/R) 
 
 
 
Res. 304-25: AUTHORIZES THE MAYOR TO ENTER INTO AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL  
AGREEMENT WITH THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF VETERANS 
AFFAIRS, PACIFIC ISLAND HEALTH CARE SYSTEM, FOR INTERAGENCY  
PUBLIC SAFETY COORDINATION  
Authorizes the Hawaiʻi Police Department to enter into a Memorandum of 
Understanding to coordinate responsibilities concerning law enforcement, emergency 
response, mutual aid, physical security, requests for assistance, and other public safety 
activities for community-based outpatient clinics located in Hilo and Kailua-Kona. 
 
 Reference: Comm. 484 
 Intr. by: Council Member Kagiwada (B/R)

BILLS FOR ORDINANCES 
 
Bill 64: INITIATES AN AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE VI, CHAPTER 2, SECTION 6-2.2,  
 (Draft 2) OF THE CHARTER OF THE COUNTY OF HAWAI‘I (2024 EDITION), RELATING 
  TO THE DIRECTOR OF PUBLIC WORKS  
Replaces the requirement that the Director of Public Works must be a registered 
professional engineer with the requirement that the Director have a bachelor’s degree in 
engineering, architecture, business, public administration or a related discipline, or be a 
licensed attorney.  Adds the requirement that at least two of the five years of required 
administrative or managerial experience must be in public works or construction, or a 
related field.  Requires the Deputy Director be a registered professional engineer and 
have authority over engineering responsibilities when the Director lacks such 
certification. 
 
   Reference: Comm. 334.18 
 Intr. by: Council Member Inaba  
 Recommitted:    August 6, 2025 
 Postponed:       September 2, 2025 
 
(Note:  There is a motion by Council Member Inaba, seconded by Council   
 Member Villegas, to recommend passage of Bill 64, as amended, on first reading.)
