Bill 1: 
 
AMENDS ORDINANCE NO. 22-63, AS AMENDED, THE OPERATING 
BUDGET FOR THE COUNTY OF HAWAI‘I FOR THE FISCAL YEAR 
ENDING JUNE 30, 2023  
Increases revenues in the Federal Grants – High Intensity Impact Grant account 
($219,900); and appropriates the same to the High Intensity Impact Grant account, 
bringing the total appropriation to $610,000.  These additional funds would be 
used by the Hawaiʻi Police Department for overtime, travel, training, services, a 
vehicle, and related equipment for its High Intensity Drug Trafficking Areas 
Program. 
 
Reference: Comm. 4 
Intr. by: Mr. Kānealiʻi-Kleinfelder (B/R) 
First Reading: December 21, 2022 
 
 
 
Bill 2: 
 
AMENDS ORDINANCE NO. 22-63, AS AMENDED, THE OPERATING 
BUDGET FOR THE COUNTY OF HAWAI‘I FOR THE FISCAL YEAR 
ENDING JUNE 30, 2023  
Increases revenues in the Federal Grants – Substance Abuse Prevention 
Coordinator account ($90,000); and appropriates the same to the Substance Abuse 
Prevention Coordinator account, bringing the total appropriation to $200,000.  
These additional funds would be used by the Office of the Prosecuting Attorney for 
substance abuse prevention systems on Hawai‘i Island. 
 
Reference: Comm. 5 
Intr. by: Mr. Kānealiʻi-Kleinfelder (B/R) 
First Reading: December 21, 2022

Comm. 32: 
 
JOEL H. WHITE V. COUNTY OF HAWAIʻI, ET AL., CASE 21-00264-JMS-RT  
From Deputy Corporation Counsel Mark D. Disher, dated December 8, 2022, 
requesting an attorney-client confidential discussion to consult with the Council 
regarding the above-referenced matter, including litigation filed by Jacob G. 
Delaplane, Esquire, on behalf of his client Joel H. White. 
 
The Council anticipates convening an executive meeting regarding the above matter, pursuant to 
Section 92-5(a)(4), for the purpose of consulting with Council’s attorney on questions and issues 
pertaining to the Council’s powers, duties, privileges, immunities, and liabilities.  A 2/3 vote pursuant to 
Hawaiʻi Revised Statutes, Section 92-4, and Rule Nos. 2 and 10 of the Rules of Procedure of the County 
Council is necessary to hold an executive meeting.
