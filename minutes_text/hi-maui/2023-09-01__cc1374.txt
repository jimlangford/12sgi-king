WALK BETWEEN CARS PARKED ON THE SIDE OF THE ROAD AND FELT CONCERN FOR THEIR 
SAFETY. A PEW YEARS BACK, DURING MY FIRST BUDGET SEASON, I ASKED COUNCIL MEMBER 
TAMARA PALTIN MAY WE COULD PUSH FOR SPEED TABLES TO BE INSTALLED ON THAT STREET. 
SHE AGREED WITH ME. NOW MY COUSIN'S HOME IS LEVELED, THE PARK'S GRASS IS BROWN, THE 
SHELLS OF BURNED CARS LAY ALONGSIDE THE ROAD.\r\n\r\nWE CAN'T GO IN THERE. NO ONE CAN 
GO IN THERE. IT'S ALL GONE. MY HEART HURTS. MY HEAD SWIRLS WITH EMOTION, RAGE, 
SADNESS, EMPATHY, I WANT TO GO BACK DO THOSE HOT LAHAINA NIGHTS AND SAVOR WHAT I 
NOW KNOW IS NO MORE, BUT THE REALITY SETS IN, GRIEVING COMES IN MANY STAGES. AND 
WHATEVER STAGE YOU'RE IN, IT'S THE CORRECT ONE. FOR ME, I'M HERE TO HELP I KNOW THIS 
BODY IS HERE TO HELP, TOO.\r\n\r\nWE HAVE WORK TO DO, MEMBERS. WHAT HAPPENED IS BEYOND  
DEVASTATING, AND FROM DAY ONE, WE HAVE BEEN HELPING THOSE AFFECTED BY THE FIRES. I 
HAVE HOPE. I HAVE SEEN HOPE, I HAVE SEEN US COME TOGETHER AS A COMMUNITY. I HOPE WE 
COME TOGETHER AS A BODY. I HOPE WE ALL HAVE EMPATHY AND UNDERSTANDING FOR 
PUAMANA, LAHAINA, KULA, AND EVERYONE ELSE AFFECTED BY THESE DEVASTATING FIRES. I 
HOPE MAHALO CHAIR.\r\n\r\nI YIELD THE FLOOR. >> THANK YOU FOR THOSE BEAUTIFUL WORDS.
