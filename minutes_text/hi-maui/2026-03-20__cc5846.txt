1. As of March 9, 2026, the National Weather Service identified an approaching Kona Low 
weather system that subsequently brought severe storm conditions to the State of Hawaiʻi 
and the County of Maui. Based upon this information, Governor Josh Green, M.D., issued 
two Emergency Proclamations on March 9, 2026, and Mayor Richard T. Bissen, Jr., issued 
an Emergency Proclamation on March 10, 2026. As a result of this extreme, unanticipated 
weather event, extensive damage to private and public property has occurred. 
 
2. In order for the County to continue its remediation efforts, an amendment is needed to 
Appendix A of the Fiscal Year 2026 Budget for the County of Maui as it pertains to Part II, 
Special Purpose Revenues – Schedule of Revolving/Special Funds for Fiscal Year 2026 
Emergency Fund. 
 
3. Without Council’s immediate action to appropriate additional funding to undertake 
restorative efforts, an imminent peril to the public health, safety, or welfare exists and will 
continue to exist until restoration and cleanup efforts are completed, and a meeting in less 
time than is provided for in Section 92-7, Hawaiʻi Revised Statutes ("HRS"), is required. 
 
4. This Emergency Meeting is held pursuant to Section 92-8, HRS. 
 
5. This Emergency Meeting is also classified as a Special Council meeting under Section 3-
5(2) Revised Charter of the County of Maui (1983), as amended. 
 
NOTE: HRS 92-8 requires adopting the findings (above) by at least 6 affirmative votes to allow 
the emergency meeting with less than 6 days as required under the Sunshine Law.
