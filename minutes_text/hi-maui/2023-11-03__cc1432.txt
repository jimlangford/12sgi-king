[ ROLL CALL ] VETERAN'S DAY IS A TIME TO THANK THOSE WHO SERVED THIS COUNTRY DURING 
TIMES OF BOTH CONFLICT AND PEACE. FOR ME, IT'S TO REMEMBER MY GRANDFATHERS ON BOTH 
MY PARENTS' SIDE WHO SERVED IN WORLD WAR I AND MY BROTHERS WHO SERVED IN WORLD 
WAR II AND KOREAN WAR AND FOR MY MANY NIECES AND NEPHEWS WHO CONTINUE TO SERVE IN  
THE U.S. MARINE CORPS, AIR FORCE, ARMY, SPECIAL FORCES, U.S. ARMY AND THE NATIONAL 
GUARD. ALL THESE SACRIFICES ARE FREELY GIVEN TO US, SO WE MAY HAVE THE RIGHTS TO 
EXERCISE EVERY DAY, SOMETIMES WITHOUT EVEN THINKING OF THAT COST. AS A CHILD AND 
SIBLING OF VETERANS, I KNOW FIRSTHAND WHAT IT COSTS. I'M GRATEFUL TO THE U.S. ARMY, 
WHO TOOK MY DAD, AS HE CALLS HIMSELF, A PUNK KID FROM KAKAKO, WHO TAUGHT HIMSELF 
DISCIPLINE AND LOYALTY AND RAISED AND TAUGHT ELEVEN CHILDREN THE SAME AND AFTER 
RETIREMENT WAS CALLED TO SERVE THE HIGHEST POWERS OF ALL. I'M DEEPLY INDEBTED TO 
AND GRATEFUL TO EVERY ENLISTED PERSONNEL, PAST, PRESENT AND FUTURE FOR THE LOVE OF
