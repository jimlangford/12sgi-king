MS. JOY GALATRO. PACIFIC BIODIESEL:
Good morning. Happy Aloha Friday. Good morning. Chair Lee and Councilmembers.
I'm Joy Galatro with Pacific Biodiesel here to give testimony on the proposed fuel taxes
for biodiesel in the Fiscal Year 2023 Budget.
First, mahalo to you, Councilmembers, for supporting the continuation of the fuel tax
exemption on B100 Biodiesel. And mahalo for not supporting the Mayor's proposed
fuel tax increase on Biodiesel Blends from six cents to eleven cents per gallon.
We're asking you, Councilmembers, to please consider an exemption of the fuel tax for
Biodiesel Blends of B20 and higher. That's something that you all had done previously
for many years. And the reason is urgency. Our climate is in crisis and we must
continue to do everything we can to reduce our consumption of fossil fuel.
Public Hearing of the Council of the County of Maui
May 13, 2022
Page 6
As a County, we need to be incentivizing the use of renewables on our roads, because
biodiesel and any blend of biodiesel can be used today to immediately reduce
greenhouse gas emissions. And that's crucial. There is no need to purchase another
vehicle. There is no need to modify the engine. Just simply switch the fuel. It’s locally
made effective and it's a climate change solution that's available right now.
So, unfortunately not choosing to fuel with biodiesel is supporting fossil fuel. So, again,
we ask you to please not tax the solution, biodiesel and other renewables. And instead,
increase the tax on the problem, fossil fuel. Penalize those who are continuing to
choose fossil fuel over renewables.
Keep the fuel tax exemption on B100. Exempt the fuel tax for Biodiesel Blends of B20
and higher. And please continue to support choosing to drive with renewables in Maui
County. Mahalo.
