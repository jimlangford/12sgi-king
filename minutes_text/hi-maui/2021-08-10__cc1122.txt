CHAIR LEE: Okay. Members the purpose of this public hearing is to receive testimony on
the proposed bill entitled "A BILL FOR AN ORDINANCE AMENDING SECTION 2.SOB,
MAUI COUNTY CODE, TO ADD 'MITIGATE CLIMATE CHANGE AND WORK
TOWARD RESILIENCE' AS A GOAL OF THE COUNTYWIDE POLICY PLAN".
As a reminder, this bill would amend the Countywide Policy Plan, so we are required
by Section 2.80B.060C., of the Maui County Code, to hold public hearings on this bill
before we can consider approving it. This public hearing serves as the public hearing
for Maui.
Before we proceed with testimony, 1 ask that everyone please keep their microphones
muted, unless it is your time to speak. Decorum shall be maintained at all times.
Mr. Clerk, please proceed with testimony.
DEPUTY COUNTY CLERK: Chair, proceeding with the presentation of testimony. Members
of the public may testify by connecting to the Council's online meeting by phone or
video conference through the information posted on today's public hearing notice.
Testimony is limited to the proposed bill posted on today's hearing notice. Individuals
may testify for three minutes. When testifying, please state your name, and the name
of any organization you represent. Please also identify to the Council if you are a paid
lobbyist.
Chair, there are currently no testifiers signed up.
CHAIR LEE: We should have this kind of unanimous support on everything we do. Of course,
we'd be dreaming, but that's okay.
We'll wait a few minutes, Mr. Clerk. Just a couple minutes. And you may want to ask
again if anybody wants to testify.
DEPUTY COUNTY CLERK: Yes, Chair. If there is anyone in the meeting who wishes to
testify, please identify yourself now. We still have no one signed up to testify.
Chair, 1 will also note that we have received written testimony for this public hearing.
CHAIR LEE: Members, any objections to receiving the written testimony into the record?

It seems a bit disingenuous to add hollow words to the already overburdened Code. After all, actions speak
louder than words. This is the County that pollutes the nearshore waters with our sewage, sets unenforceable
goals to limit the hordes of ill-mannered visitors, speaks of resilience when our prime agricultural land again
goes to marketable crops that have little to do with food resilience for our residents, and, recently, constructed
perhaps the largest heat island in the state by repaving the nearly 400 stall War Memorial parking lot with black
asphalt while completely ignoring any consideration of its contribution to climate change as well as its own
rules requiring one shade tree for every 5 stalls. Time to lead by example. Time for true action, not feel good
wordsmithing.
