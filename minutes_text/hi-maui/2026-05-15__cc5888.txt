BUDGET, FINANCE, AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT COMMITTEE: 
 
CR NO. 26-25 Recommending the following: 
 
1. FIRST READING of Bill 55, CD1 (2026), to appropriate estimated revenues 
for the operating and capital improvement budget for the Fiscal Year July 1, 
2026, to June 30, 2027.  Incorporated within Bill 55, CD1 are Appendix A, which 
lists Grants and Restricted Use Revenues and Special Purpose Revenues; 
Appendix B, which is the Schedule of Revenues – Fees, Rates, Assessments and 
Taxes; Appendix B-1, which lists the County’s concessions, leases, and licenses 
by department; and Appendix C, which provides titles and descriptions of Capital 
Improvement Projects ("Budget bill"); 
 
2. FIRST READING of Bill 56, CD1 (2026), to set the Capital Program for 
Fiscal Years 2027 through 2032.  Incorporated within Bill 56, CD1 are Appendix 
A-1, a report entitled “COUNTY OF MAUI, CAPITAL IMPROVEMENT PROJECTS 
REPORT, AS OF DECEMBER 31, 2025,” which lists all pe nding capital 
improvement projects and appropriations, except for Water Fund projects, as of 
December 31, 2025; Appendix A -2, a report entitled “County of Maui, Capital 
Improvement Program by District Job and Index, Fiscal Year Ending 6/30/2026 
– as of 12/31/2025,” which includes pending Water Fund capital improvement 
projects and appropriations as of December 31, 2025; and Appendix B, which is 
the proposed Capital Program for Fiscal Years 2027 through 2032 ("Capital 
Program bill");  
 
3. FIRST READING  of Bill 57, CD1 (2026), to authorize the issuance of 
$193,453,400 in general obligation bonds for various capital improvement 
projects ("Bond Authorization bill"); 
 
4. FIRST READING  of Bill 58, CD1 (2026), to reauthorize the use of 
$6,000,000 in lapsed bond proceeds for the Department of the Prosecuting 
Attorney, Wailuku-Kahului Community Plan Area, Government Facilities, for 24 
North Church Street Building Renovations ("Lapsed Bond bill"); 
 
5. FIRST READING of Bill 59, CD1 (2026), to authorize the Mayor to enter 
into an intergovernmental agreement to finance the Kīhei Wastewater Pump 
Station No. 7 Relocation, with funds not to exceed $2,500,000; 
 
6. FIRST READING of Bill 60, CD1 (2026), to authorize the Mayor to enter 
into an intergovernmental agreement to finance Countywide Facility 
Improvements project, with funds not to exceed $10,000,000; 
 4 
 
7. FIRST READING  of Bill 62, CD1  (2026), to:  (1) allow the Solid Waste 
Management Fund to be used for recycling programs and remove recycling 
programs as an authorized use of the Environmental Protection and 
Sustainability Fund; and (2) expand the uses of the Solid Waste Management 
Fund; 
8. FIRST READING of Bill 63, CD1 (2026), to rename the Hawaiian Cultural 
Restoration Revolving Fund to the Lahaina Royal Complex Restoration Fund and 
authorize the Fund to be used for preservation and restoration throughout the 
Lahaina Royal Complex area; 
 
9. FIRST READING of Bill 64, CD1  (2026), to establish a fee for the use of 
County-owned electric vehicle charging stations to offset the cost of electricity 
consumed through the stations; 
 
10. FIRST READING  of Bill 76, CD1  (2026), to amend the Water System 
Development Fee by basing the fee on projected average daily water use instead 
of water meter size and count; 
 
11. ADOPTION of Resolution 26-63, to adopt the fuel tax rates for the County 
of Maui, effective July 1, 2026, in accordance with Section  243-5, Hawai‘i 
Revised Statutes, and Section 3.20.010, Maui County Code; 
 
12. REFERRAL of Bill 61 (2026), to establish a Fiscal Reserve Fund that the 
County may use as a temporary funding source during financial hardship to help 
reduce the need for tax increases or service cuts, to your Budget, Finance, and 
Economic Development Committee; 
 
13. REFERRAL of Bill 66 (2026), to establish the Climate Action and 
Resiliency Revolving Fund, which would support the implementation of the 
Climate Action and Resiliency Plan adopted under Code Chapter 20.44, with at 
least 20 percent of the Maui County Transient Accommodations Tax collected  to 
be deposited into the Fund, to your Agriculture, Diversification, Environment, 
and Public Transportation Committee; and 
 
14. REFERRAL of Bill 67 (2026), to enhance stewardship of the County’s 
natural, cultural, and agricultural resources by requiring that 20  percent of 
Maui County Transient Accommodations Tax revenues be deposited into the 
Environmental Protection and Sustainability Fun d and that specified 
consultations occur before annual expenditures of those revenues, to your 
Agriculture, Diversification, Environment, and Public Transportation 
Committee.
