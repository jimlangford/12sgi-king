MS. KEHUS: Alrighty. So, aloha and mahalo. County Councilmembers and Chair Lee. My
name is Roxann Kehus and I am the program specialist for the Hawaii State Council
on Developmental Disabilities. The mission of the DD Council is to empower,
advocate, and support individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities
statewide to control their own destiny and determine the quality of life they desire.
So, historically, people with developmental and intellectual disabilities have been
treated poorly. They've been taken away from their families and communities, sent to
live in institutions, denied education, called names, and sadly, undervalued. But in my
lifetime, institutions have closed, laws have been passed to right the many wrongs
committed against people with developmental disabilities.
Thirty-four years ago, in 1987, President Reagan designated March of each year to be
Developmental Disabilities Awareness Month. Progress has been made to embrace
the gifts and talents of individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities. We
know that it's not about being special. It's about having equality and safeguards in
place for people to participate and contribute to the community, and not be set apart,
or separated, or isolated. People with intellectual developmental disabilities have a
voice and are strong advocates for their rights. They speak up for themselves. They
are friends in our community.
During this time of COVID-19, self-advocates have been volunteering to feed the poor
and the hungry. They've been doing beach cleanup and adopting highways to keep
