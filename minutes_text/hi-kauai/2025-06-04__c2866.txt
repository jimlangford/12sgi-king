Council Services Division at 4396 Rice Street, Suite 209, Lihu‘e, Hawai‘i, 96766; or hand-
delivery at Suite 209 during regular business hours (7:45 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday through

Courtesy Livestream, Video, and Minutes:
 The meeting will be streamed live, with the recorded
video thereafter available, at 
www.kauai.gov/Government/Council/Webcast-Meetings
. If the
courtesy broadcast connection cannot be maintained or is otherwise unavailable, the meeting will
proceed. Meeting minutes will be completed pursuant to Hawai‘i Revised Statutes Chapter 92 and
electronically posted on the Council's website at 
www.kauai.gov/government/council
 following
Council approval.
Courtesy Remote Oral Testimony:
 This is 
not
 a remote meeting pursuant to HRS Section 92-3.7.
The option of remote oral testimony is offered as a courtesy only. To register, testifiers shall sign
up via emailing 
remote.signup@kauai.gov
, indicating the agenda item(s) they would like to
provide testimony on. Emails are due no later than 12:00 Noon the business day prior to the
meeting. Those who timely register will receive remote testimony access and instructions via
reply email. Those wishing to view the meeting but not provide testimony will be directed to the
courtesy livestream (via the website link above). If the courtesy remote testimony access is
unavailable for any reason, the meeting will proceed.
Additional Note for Both Methods of Oral Testimony:
 Generally, each agenda item is addressed
(and oral testimony received) in the order listed in the agenda; however, agenda items may occur
out of order without notice and oral testimony is not accepted for completed agenda items. Those
seeking the guaranteed ability to testify should report: (1) in-person, and (2) by the meeting start
time.

COMMUNICATIONS:
1.
C 2025-133
 
Communication (05/22/2025) from the Executive on Aging, requesting Council
approval to receive and expend $23,439.00 in Federal funds, for Fiscal Year 2025 for the
second and third allotment of the Nutrition Services Incentive Program (NSIP) to purchase
food for the congregate and home-delivered meals program, and to indemnify the State
Executive Office on Aging.
2.
C 2025-134
 
Communication (05/22/2025) from the Executive on Transportation, requesting
Council approval to apply for, receive, expend, and indemnify the Federal Transit
Administration (FTA) for Section 5311 CMAQ flexible funds in the amount of
$5,000,000.00 for four (4) electric buses, to replace four (4) diesel and gas buses which are
soon to be beyond their useful lives, to be used to fund eighty-five percent (85%) of the
purchase cost, and fifteen percent (15%) from the Transportation Agency’s Fiscal Year 2026
budget request.
3.
C 2025-135
 
Communication (05/23/2025) from Acting Assistant Chief of Police Makana
Rivera, requesting Council approval of the indemnification provisions contained in the
Magnet Forensics End User License Agreement (EULA), for renewal of the AXIOM
software license to allow the Kaua‘i Police Department (KPD) to remain equipped with
advanced forensic tools that support KPD’s commitment to effective and timely criminal
investigations.
4.
C 2025-136
 
Communication (05/27/2025) from Acting Assistant Chief of Police Makana
Rivera, requesting Council approval of the indemnification provisions contained in the
Cellebrite End Use License Agreement (EULA), for renewal of the Cellebrite license to
allow the Kaua‘i Police Department (KPD) to use software to secure data from a mobile
device and to access software updates required for court admissibility of product usage.
5.
C 2025-137
 
Communication (05/27/2025) from the Executive on Aging, requesting Council
approval to receive and expend State funds in the amount of $102,107.25, and to indemnify
the State Executive Office on Aging, for the Healthy Aging Partnership (HAP), which will be
used towards Stanford Chronic Disease Self-Management Education and Evidence-Based
Fall Prevention Programs for the period July 1, 2025 through June 30, 2026.
