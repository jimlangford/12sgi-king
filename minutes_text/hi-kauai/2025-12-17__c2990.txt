MEETING INFORMATION:
Oral Testimony:
 Accepted in-person at the Council Chambers.
Written Testimony:
 May be submitted for any meeting via: email to
counciltestimony@kauai.gov
; fax to (808) 241-6349; mail to the Office of the County Clerk,
Council Services Division at 4396 Rice Street, Suite 209, Lihu‘e, Hawai‘i, 96766; or hand-
delivery at Suite 209 during regular business hours (7:45 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday through
Friday, excluding holidays).
Courtesy Livestream, Video, and Minutes:
 The meeting will be streamed live, with the recorded
video thereafter available, at 
https://www.kauai.gov/Government/Council/Webcast-Meetings
. If
the courtesy broadcast connection cannot be maintained or is otherwise unavailable, the meeting
will proceed. Meeting minutes will be completed pursuant to Hawai‘i Revised Statutes Chapter
92 and electronically posted on the Council's website at 
www.kauai.gov/government/council
following Council approval.
Courtesy Remote Oral Testimony:
 This is 
not
 a remote meeting pursuant to HRS Section 92-3.7.
The option of remote oral testimony is offered as a courtesy only. To register, testifiers shall sign
up via emailing 
remote.signup@kauai.gov
, indicating the agenda item(s) they would like to
provide testimony on. Emails are due no later than 12:00 Noon the business day prior to the
meeting. Those who timely register will receive remote testimony access and instructions via
reply email. Those wishing to view the meeting but not provide testimony will be directed to the
courtesy livestream (via the website link above). If the courtesy remote testimony access is
unavailable for any reason, the meeting will proceed.
Additional Note for Both Methods of Oral Testimony:
 Generally, each agenda item is addressed
(and oral testimony received) in the order listed in the agenda; however, agenda items may occur
out of order without notice and oral testimony is not accepted for completed agenda items. Those
seeking the guaranteed ability to testify should report: (1) in-person, and (2) by the meeting start
time.

cokcouncil@kauai.gov
 as soon as
possible. Requests made as early as possible will allow adequate time to fulfill your request.

C 2025-255
 
Communication (11/28/2025) from the Director of Finance, requesting Council
approval to apply for, receive, and expend State and Local Cybersecurity Grant Program (SLCGP)
funds, in the amount of $389,500.00, from the United States Department of Homeland Security (DHS),
via the State of Hawai‘i Office of Homeland Security, to support efforts to enhance cybersecurity
capabilities, implementing a modern cloud-native Network Access Control (NAC) platform to protect
the County’s infrastructure from unauthorized or insecure device access.
 
C 2025-256
 
Communication (12/04/2025) from Mark T. Ozaki, Deputy Chief of Police,
requesting Council approval of the indemnification clause included in the Versaterm Terms of Use and
Services, supporting internal affairs, professional standards, and intelligence functions critical to public
safety operations; granting a limited license to use these services and outlining responsibilities related
to system access, data handing, and legal protections, ensuring the Kaua‘i Police Department can use
the software securely and in compliance with applicable laws.
 
C 2025-257
 
Communication (12/08/2025) from Councilmember Cowden, transmitting for
Council consideration, A Bill For An Ordinance Amending Chapter 8, Kaua‘i County Code 1987, As
Amended, Relating To Uses In Districts, to create new definitions for “Employee dormitory” and
“Group housing dormitory,” respectively update the Use Table in Section 8-2.4, and outline two (2)
new sections describing application requirements that are customized to these new dormitory types.
(See Proposed Draft Bill (No. 2979))
 
C 2025-258
 
Communication (12/08/2025) from Makana A. Rivera, Assistant Chief of Police,
Support Services Bureau, requesting Council approval of the indemnification provision in the Samsara
VG55 Terms of Service for vehicle gateways, which were selected by the Kaua‘i Police Department due
to their compatibility with FirstNet and their robust telematics platform meeting the need for real-time
vehicle data and fleet analytics, which are critical to improving operational efficiency, safety, and
compliance.

EXECUTIVE SESSION:
