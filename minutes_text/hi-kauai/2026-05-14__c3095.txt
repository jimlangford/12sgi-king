The schedule below relates to the review, deliberation, and decision-making of 
the Council and its Finance & Economic Development Committee concerning the 
Mayor's Annual Budget for Fiscal Year 2026-2027, which collectively consists of 
Bill No. 2988, "A Bill For An Ordinance Relating To The Operating Budget And 
Financing Thereof For The Fiscal Year July 1, 2026 to June 30, 2027"; Bill No. 2989, 
"A Bill For An Ordinance Relating To Capital Improvements And Financing Thereof 
For The Fiscal Year July 1, 2026 to June 30, 2027"; and Resolution No. 2026-11, 
"Resolution Establishing The Real Property Tax Rates For The Fiscal Year 
July 1, 2026 to June 30, 2027 For The County of Kaua'i." 
DELIBERATION AND PRELIMINARY DECISION-MAKING 
BY THE COUNCIL'S FINANCE & ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT COMMITTEE 
ON THE MAYOR'S 2026-2027 ANNUAL BUDGET 
WILL BE HELD, AS NECESSARY, ON THE FOLLOWING DATES AND TIMES: 
Thursday, May 14, 2026 
Friday, May 15, 2026 
9:00 a.m. - 4:30 p.m. 
(as determined by the Council's Finance & Economic Development Committee Chair) 
The purpose of Deliberation and Preliminary Decision-Making is the 
deliberation and preliminary decision-making regarding various positions, specific 
budget revenue or appropriation items, budget provisos, and real property tax rates, 
which could result in amendments to Bill No. 2988, Bill No. 2989, and Resolution 
No. 2026-11. The Finance & Economic Development Committee may also consider 
the following in its Deliberation and Preliminary Decision-Making on the Mayor's 
2026-2027 Annual Budget: (1) the Mayor's Supplemental Budget Modification 
Communication to the Council; (2) items discussed in Departmental Budget Reviews; 
(3) items discussed in Departmental Call-Backs; and (4) items relating to the budget, 
including, but not limited to, use of a line-item or program budget format. 
Meeting dates listed after May 14, 2026 are a continuation of the May 14, 2026 
meeting. Meetings are subject to cancelation. Interested persons may contact the 
Council Services Division to determine the status of any meeting. 
MEETING INFORMATION: 
• This is an in-person meeting at multiple meeting sites connected by interactive 
conference technology pursuant to Hawai'i Revised Statutes (HRS) Section 92-3.5. 
Councilmembers will attend the meeting at the Council Chambers location only. 
Members of the public may attend the meeting at either location. 
• In-Person Attendance: Unless otherwise noted, all meetings concerning the 
Mayor's Annual Budget will be held at the Council Chambers, 4396 Rice Street, 
Suite 201, and Meeting Room 106, Historic County Building, Lihu'e, Hawai'i, 
96766, at 9:00 a.m. or soon thereafter. 
• Oral Testimony: Accepted in-person at the Council Chambers and Meeting 
Room 106.

• Courtesy Remote Oral Testimony: This is not a remote meeting pursuant to 
HRS Section 92-3. 7. The option of remote oral testimony is offered as a courtesy 
only. To register, testifiers shall sign up via emailing remote.si gnup@kauai. gov, 
indicating that they would like to provide testimony on the budget proceeding for 
the specified day. Emails are due no later than 12:00 Noon the business day prior 
to the meeting. Those who timely register will receive remote testimony access 
and instructions via reply email. Those wishing to view the meeting but not 
provide testimony will be directed to the courtesy livestream (via the website link 
above). If the courtesy remote testimony access is unavailable for any reason, the 
meeting will proceed. 
• Additional Note for Both Methods of Oral Testimony: Generally, budget 
public testimony will be accepted at the beginning of each day (subject to change). 
Those seeking the guaranteed ability to testify should report: (1) in-person, 
and (2) by the meeting start time.

ADDITIONAL NOTIFICATION -NO ELEVATOR ACCESS TO SECOND FLOOR: 
At the time of this meeting notice and agenda posting, the Historic County 
Building elevator is temporarily inoperable. Because the Council Chambers is on the 
second floor of the building, an in-person meeting site at Meeting Room 106 will be 
connected to the in-person meeting site at the Council Chambers by interactive 
conference technology. If audio communication to the meeting cannot be maintained 
between the locations, the meeting shall be automatically recessed to restore 
communication. If the audio communication cannot be restored within thirty (30) 
minutes, the meeting will be terminated. If the audiovisual communication can be 
restored, the meeting may reconvene. If the audio communication can be restored, 
the meeting may reconvene with audio-only communication, with hardcopies of visual 
aids made available to all meeting participants pursuant to HRS Section 92-3.5(c).
