MEETING INFORMATION:
Oral Testimony:
 Accepted in-person at the Council Chambers.
Written Testimony:
 May be submitted before any meeting via: email to
counciltestimony@kauai.gov
; fax to (808) 241-6349; mail to the Office of the County Clerk,
Council Services Division at 4396 Rice Street, Suite 209, Lihu‘e, Hawai‘i, 96766; or hand-
delivery at Suite 209 during regular business hours (7:45 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday through
Friday, excluding holidays).
Courtesy Livestream, Video, and Minutes:
 The meeting will be streamed live, with the recorded
video thereafter available, at 
www.kauai.gov/Government/Council/Webcast-Meetings
. If the
courtesy broadcast connection cannot be maintained or is otherwise unavailable, the meeting will
proceed. Meeting minutes will be completed pursuant to Hawai‘i Revised Statutes Chapter 92 and
electronically posted on the Council's website at 
www.kauai.gov/government/council
 following
Council approval.
Courtesy Remote Oral Testimony:
 This is 
not
 a remote meeting pursuant to HRS Section 92-3.7.
The option of remote oral testimony is offered as a courtesy only.  To register, testifiers shall sign
up via emailing 
remote.signup@kauai.gov
, indicating the agenda item(s) they would like to
provide testimony on.  Registration emails are due no later than 12:00 Noon the day prior to the
meeting.  Those who timely register will receive remote testimony access and instructions via
reply email.  Those wishing to view the meeting but not provide testimony will be directed to the
courtesy livestream (via the website link above).  If the courtesy remote testimony access is
unavailable for any reason, the meeting will proceed. 
Additional Note for Both Methods of Oral Testimony:
 Generally, each agenda item is addressed
(and oral testimony received) in the order listed in the agenda; however, agenda items may occur
out of order without notice and oral testimony is not accepted for completed agenda items. Those
seeking the guaranteed ability to testify should report: (1) in-person, and (2) by the meeting start
time.

cokcouncil@kauai.gov
 as soon as
possible. Requests made as early as possible will allow adequate time to fulfill your request.

C 2023-250
 
Communication (11/22/2023) from the Deputy Director of Planning, requesting
Council approval to apply for, receive, and expend  National Oceanic and Atmospheric
Administration (NOAA) Coastal Zone Management (CZM) Habitat Protection and
Restoration Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL) Competition Grant funds in the amount of
$409,500.00, and to indemnify the State of Hawai‘i, to be used for Habitat Restoration
Planning, Engineering, and Design for the Historic Salt Pans at Hanapepe, Kaua‘i, Hawai‘i.
 The project aims to develop and implement immediate design solutions to projected
coastal impacts, such as dune restoration and other mitigative measures, and identify long-
term solutions to preserve the hydrogeologically unique, historically significant, and actively
cultivated wahi pana.

C 2023-251
 
Communication (11/22/2023) from the Deputy Director of Planning, requesting
Council approval to apply for, receive, and expend National Oceanic and Atmospheric
Administration (NOAA) Climate Resiliency Regional Challenge Grant funds in the amount
of $1,068,510.00, and to indemnify NOAA, to establish a Climate-Resilient Kaua‘i
Collaborative with three (3) staff over a three-year period. The objective of the program is to
build partnerships, engage stakeholders, secure funding, and empower coastal communities
with the knowledge needed to adapt in the face of climate change.
