   HONOLULU CITY COUNCIL KE KANIHELA O KE KALANA O HONOLULU CITY AND COUNTY OF HONOLULU   

   COMMITTEE ON ZONING 

 Voting Members: Calvin K.Y. Say, Chair Tyler Dos Santos-Tam, Vice Chair Radiant Cordero Esther Kiaʻāina Matt Weyer 

 AGENDA 

 REGULAR MEETING 

 CITY COUNCIL CHAMBER 

 WEDNESDAY, AUGUST 23, 2023 

 9:00 A.M. 

 ORAL TESTIMONY 

 Oral testimony will be permitted on all items on the agenda, subject to the following restrictions. Each speaker may not have anyone else read their statement and is limited to a one‑minute presentation. 

 Remote Testimony 

 1.  For direct access to submit oral testimony call: +1-253-215-8782, enter ID:  87599449081 and Passcode: 342117 . 

 2.  To testify by videoconference visit: https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/testimony . Videoconference access information will be provided upon registration. Testifiers are encouraged to register/submit testimony at least 24 hours prior to the meeting. 

 Although remote oral testimony is being permitted, this is a regular meeting and not a remote meeting by interactive conference technology under HRS Section 92-3.7. Therefore, the meeting will continue notwithstanding loss of audiovisual communication with remote testifiers or loss of the public broadcast of the meeting. 

 In-Person Testimony in the Council Chamber 

 Persons wishing to testify are requested to register by 9:00 a.m. by filling out the registration form in person outside the Council Chamber. Persons who have not registered will be given an opportunity to speak following the oral testimonies of the registered speakers. 

 Remote and in-person oral testimony will be allowed when each agenda item is taken up, in the following order: 

 1.  Remote testimony; 

 2.  In-person testimony in the Council Chamber.

 WRITTEN TESTIMONY 

 Written testimony may be uploaded at https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/testimony , or mailed to Office of the City Clerk, Attention: Information Section, 530 South King Street, Room 100, Honolulu, HI 96813. If submitted, written testimonies, including the testifier’s address, e-mail address, and phone number, will be available to the public at https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov . 

 Should you have any questions, please call (808) 768-3816 or send an email to kiana.pascual@honolulu.gov . 

 * * * * * * 

 MATERIALS AVAILABLE FOR INSPECTION 

 Meeting materials (“ board packet ” under HRS Section 92-7.5) are accessible at https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/browse/agendas by clicking on the appropriate Committee meeting. 

 If you need auxiliary aid/service or other accomodation due to a disability or an interpreter for a language other than English, please call the Office of the City Clerk Information Section at (808) 768-5822 between 7:45 a.m. and 4:30 p.m. or send an email to kiana.pascual@honolulu.gov as soon as possible or at least three (3) business days before the scheduled meeting. Requests made as early as possible have a greater likelihood of being fulfilled. 

 VIEWING THE MEETING 

 The meeting will be viewable: (1) by internet live streaming through https://www.honolulucitycouncil.org/meetings ; (2) by televised live broadcast on ‘Ōlelo TV Channel 54; and (3) on the monitor situated outside the Council Chamber. Viewers who experience a loss of viewing signal should try switching to another viewing option. 

 After the meeting, the meeting will be viewable on demand at https://www.honolulucitycouncil.org/meetings . Copies of older meeting videos may be requested by calling the City Clerk’s Office at (808) 768‑5822, charges may apply.

 PLANNING AND PERMITTING - Draft Resolution requesting for exemption from Park Dedication, Hālawa View Apartments, Resolution No. 20-188, CD1, Hālawa View Housing Partners LP, 99-009 Kalaloa Street - Hālawa, Tax Map Key 9-9-003: 026.

 M-0453(23) 

 R.M. TOWILL CORPORATION - Presentation on RES23-183 for August 23, 2023 @ 9:00 AM Zoning (ZON) meeting.

 M-0472(23) 

 Testimony on RES23-183 for Aug 23, 2023 @ 09:00 AM ZONING (ZON) Meeting.

 PROPOSED CD1 TO RESOLUTION 23-183 (Submitted by Councilmember Say) – The CD1 (OCS2023-0751/8/17/2023 10:04 AM) makes the following amendments: 

 A. Amends the resolution title to read as follows: 

 "AMENDING RESOLUTION 20-188, CD1, AS AMENDED BY RESOLUTION 22-164, CD1, TO PROVIDE AN EXEMPTION FROM CERTAIN PARK DEDICATION REQUIREMENTS FOR THE HĀLAWA VIEW APARTMENTS AFFORDABLE HOUSING PROJECT DEVELOPED PURSUANT TO § 201H-38, HAWAII REVISED STATUTES." 

 B. In the first WHEREAS clause, clarifies the description of the Project. 

 C. Moves the fifth WHEREAS clause (relating to the execution and recordation of the 201H Agreement) to the second WHEREAS clause, and provides that the 201H Agreement was executed on January 17, 2023, and recorded with the State Bureau of Conveyances on March 23, 2023. 

 D. Adds a new third WHEREAS clause to provide that pursuant to the 201H Agreement, the Applicant is required to provide 300 affordable rental units to be rented to households earning 60 percent and below of the AMI, and two manager units in a 25-story residential tower in Phases 2 and 3 of the Project (the "affordable residential tower"); and 156 market-rate rental units in a 25-story residential tower in Phase 4 of the Project (the "market‑rate residential tower"). 

 Page 6 

 E. Adds a new fourth WHEREAS clause to describe two minor modifications to the Project that were approved by the DPP. 

 F. In the sixth WHEREAS clause, provides that the building permits for the Project's foundation and superstructure (building shell) were issued on March 28, 2023, and April 17, 2023, respectively. 

 G. Adds a new eighth WHEREAS clause to provide that in June 2023, the Applicant began construction of Phases 2 and 3 of the Project, which includes foundation work for the Project's parking podium and affordable residential tower. 

 H. Adds a new ninth WHEREAS clause to provide that under Resolution 20‑188, CD1, the Applicant did not request an exemption from park dedication requirements, and instead intended to provide a combination of private onsite park and playground areas (as depicted on Exhibits 1 through 6) and the payment of an in-lieu fee to satisfy the Project's park dedication requirements. 

 I. In the tenth WHEREAS clause, clarifies that upon submitting a park dedication application to the DPP for compliance with ROH Chapter 22, Article 7, various Project site constraints were identified, which severely limited the available park space on the Project site that would meet the standards under the City's Park Dedication Rules and Regulations. 

 J. Adds a new eleventh WHEREAS clause to set forth the four primary constraints that limited the available park and playground areas on the Project site. 

 K. In the twelfth WHEREAS clause, clarifies that Project development costs have drastically increased due to higher interest rates, inflation, and higher construction costs, making it difficult for the Applicant to pay park dedication requirement in-lieu fees. 

 L. In the thirteenth WHEREAS clause, provides that the Applicant has requested an exemption from park dedication requirements attributed to 300 (instead of 302) affordable rental units to be rented to households earning 60 percent and below of the AMI, totaling 33,000 square feet of park space or a payment of an equivalent in-lieu fee estimated at $4,257,000  (instead of $4,285,380). 

 Page 7 

 M. Adds a new fourteenth WHEREAS clause to provide that the Applicant will satisfy the park dedication requirements attributed to 156 market-rate rental units and two manager units, totaling 17,600 square feet of park space or payment of an equivalent in-lieu fee estimated $2,270,400, by providing a combination of onsite park and playground areas and an in-lieu fee payment. 

 N. In the last WHEREAS clause, clarifies that the Council finds good cause to authorize an exemption from park dedication requirements attributed to 300 affordable rental units to be rented to households earning 60 percent and below of the AMI. 

 O. In the BE IT RESOLVED clause, provides that Resolution 20-188, CD1, as amended by Resolution 22-164, CD1, is amended by adding a new Exemption 15, to read in its entirety as follows: 

 " Park Dedication Ordinance Requirements : 

 15. Exemption from ROH Chapter 22, Article 7, to allow an exemption from park dedication ordinance requirements attributed to 300 affordable units to be rented to households earning 60 percent and below of the AMI, totaling 33,000 square feet of park space, or payment of an equivalent in-lieu fee, estimated at $4,257,000." 

 P. In the first BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED clause, provides that Resolution 20-188, CD1, as amended by Resolution 22-164, CD1, is further amended by adding a new Condition K to read in its entirety as follows: 

 "K. All onsite park and playground areas provided to satisfy a portion of the Project's park dedication requirements and other onsite recreational areas must be available for use by all residents of the Project and their guests (residents that are renters or lessees of the affordable rental units and the market-rate rental units)." 

 Q. In the second BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED clause, provides that prior to the issuance of any subsequent building permit for the affordable residential tower in Project Phases 2 and 3, the Applicant and the City shall execute an amendment (or an amendment and restatement) to the 201H Agreement, dated January 17, 2023, to reflect the addition of Exemption 15 and Condition K, as set forth above, and to correct erroneous references to the market-rate units as condominium units (Resolution 20‑188, CD1, as amended by Resolution 22-164, CD1, 

 Page 8 

 identifies the market-rate units as rental units), which amendment (or amendment and restatement) must be recorded with the State Bureau of Conveyances, with a copy of the recorded document filed with the Director of Planning and Permitting. 

 R. Adds a third BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED clause to provide that all other provisions of Resolution 20-188, CD1, as amended by Resolution 22‑164,CD1, are ratified and confirmed, and remain in full force and effect. 

 S. Renumbers the exhibits as Exhibits 1 through 6 (instead of pages 1 through 6 of Exhibit 1). 

 T. Makes miscellaneous technical and nonsubstantive amendments.
