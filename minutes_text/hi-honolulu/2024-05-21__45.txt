 4.  RESOLUTION 24-102 - MISCELLANEOUS CHARTER AMENDMENTS. Initiating miscellaneous amendments to the Revised Charter of the City and County of Honolulu 1973 (Revised 2017 Edition), as amended. (Resolution passed first reading 4/17/24) 

 DEFERRED TO DATE AND TIME DETERMINED BY COMMITTEE CHAIR

 REMOTE AND IN-PERSON TESTIFIERS 

 VOTE 

 (ADDENDUM) PROPOSED CD1 TO RESOLUTION 24-102 (Submitted by Councilmember Dos Santos-Tam) -The CD1 (OCS2024-0403/4/22/2024 2:34 PM) makes the following amendments: 

 C. Amends the title to read as follows:

 "INITIATING MISCELLANEOUS AMENDMENTS TO THE REVISED CHARTER OF THE CITY AND COUNTY OF HONOLULU 1973 (AMENDED 2017 EDITION), AS AMENDED." 

 D. Amends the third WHEREAS clause to revise the description of proposed Charter amendment (1) to reflect the changes to the Charter question (see C. below).

 E. Amends the RESOLVED clause as follows:

 1. Revises section 1, the description of the proposed Charter amendment, by amending the description of Charter amendment proposal (1) to read as follows:

 "(1) authorizing the Fire Commission, the Board of Water Supply, and the Board of the Honolulu Authority for Rapid Transportation to establish terms of office for the administrative heads of the Fire Department (the Fire Chief), the Board of Water Supply (the Manager and Chief Engineer), and the Honolulu Authority for Rapid Transportation (the Executive Director), respectively;" 

 2. Amends sections 2, 5 and 10, amending Charter Sections 6-1003, 7-105, and 17-104, respectively, to provide that the Fire Commission, the Board of Water Supply, and the Board of the Honolulu Authority for Rapid Transportation may establish a term of office for the Fire Chief, the Chief Engineer of the Board of Water Supply ("BWS"), and the Executive Director of the Honolulu Authority for Rapid Transportation ("HART"), respectively, and that if a term of office is established for one of these officers, it may be established by resolution or contract and may not exceed five years (rather than specifying that the term for each position be five years).

 3. Amends section 9, which enacts a new Charter Section 16-__ (transition provisions) to provide that the Fire Chief, Chief Engineer of the BWS, and the Executive Director of HART who are in office on December 31, 2024 (the day before the effective date of the Charter amendments) may continue to serve out any term to which they were appointed prior to December 31, 2024 (rather than that they would automatically be permitted to serve for five additional years). If however, the officer’s term expires on or before December 31, 2024, they will have to be reappointed in order to continue to serve on or after January 1, 2025.

 F. Makes miscellaneous technical and non-substantive amendments.

 (ADDENDUM) PROPOSED CD1 TO RESOLUTION 24-102 (Submitted by Councilmember Dos Santos-Tam) -The CD1 (OCS2024-0524/5/16/2024 12:25 PM) makes the following amendments: 

 A. Amends the title to read as follows:

 "INITIATING MISCELLANEOUS AMENDMENTS TO THE REVISED CHARTER OF THE CITY AND COUNTY OF HONOLULU 1973 (AMENDED 2017 EDITION), AS AMENDED." 

 B. Amends the Resolution by deleting Sections 2, 5, and 10 relating to the terms of office of the Fire Chief, Chief Engineer of the Board of Water Supply, and the Executive Director of the Honolulu Authority for Rapid Transportation, respectively, and by deleting Section 9 of the Resolution that would have provided transition provisions for the terms of office of these officers. Renumbers subsequent sections accordingly.

 C. Due to the deletions referred to in B above, amends the third WHEREAS clause to delete item (1) and renumbers the subsequent items accordingly.

 D. Due to the deletions referred to in B above, amends the Charter amendment question to delete item (1) and renumbers the subsequent items accordingly.

 E. Amends Section 3 of the Resolution (now renumbered as Section 2) relating to the duties of the Director of Planning and Permitting to specify that the Director shall prepare rules and regulations relating to implementation, interpretation, and enforcement of the various City construction codes, including the Building Energy Conservation Code, and the grading, sediment control and erosion ordinances, but retain the Council’s current authority to propose amendments to the various City construction codes.

 F. Makes miscellaneous technical and non-substantive amendments.

 5.  BILL 8 (2024) - COMMUNITY WORKFORCE AGREEMENTS. Addressing community workforce agreements. (Bill passed second reading and public hearing held on 3/25/24; Committee postponed action on the Bill 4/2/24) 

 AMENDED TO CD1

 REMOTE AND IN-PERSON TESTIFIERS 

 VOTE FOR CD1 

 PROPOSED CD1 TO BILL 8 (2024) (Submitted by Councilmember Dos Santos-Tam) -The CD1 (OCS2024-0513/5/14/2024 2:52 PM) makes the following amendments: 

 REPORTED OUT FOR 3RD READING 

 A. Amends the definition of "Covered Project" to clarify that it includes major repair or rehabilitation of an existing structure, building, or facility, or existing infrastructure, that increases the value or useful life, or both, of the structure, building, facility, or infrastructure, or a replacement that is of significantly improved quality and higher value compared to the replaced item, and in either case, that is funded by an appropriation in the executive capital budget.

 B. Amends the definition of "Maintenance" to:

 (1) Clarify that it includes the upkeep of a structure, building, or facility, or of infrastructure, to preserve the useful life of the structure, building, facility, or infrastructure; and 

 (2) Delete the proposed new ordinance language, which would have provided that the definition does not include any covered projects funded by the capital budget of the city. 

 C. Deletes the proposed amendment to the definition of "Operation," which would have provided that the definition does not include any covered projects funded by the capital budget of the city.

 D. Makes miscellaneous technical and non-substantive amendments.
