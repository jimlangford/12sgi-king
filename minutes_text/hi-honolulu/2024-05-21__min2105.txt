 ORAL TESTIMONY 

 Oral testimony will be permitted on all items on the agenda, subject to the following restrictions. Each speaker may not have anyone else read their statement and is limited to a one‑minute presentation. 

 Remote Testimony 

 1.  For direct access to submit oral testimony call: +1-253-215-8782, enter ID:  84531305078 and Passcode: 104410 . 

 2.  To testify by videoconference visit: https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/testimony . Videoconference access information will be provided upon registration. Testifiers are encouraged to register/submit testimony at least 24 hours prior to the meeting. 

 Although remote oral testimony is being permitted, this is a regular meeting and not a remote meeting by interactive conference technology under HRS Section 92-3.7. Therefore, the meeting will continue notwithstanding loss of audiovisual communication with remote testifiers or loss of the public broadcast of the meeting. 

 In-Person Testimony in the Council Chamber 

 Persons wishing to testify are requested to register by 9:00 a.m. by filling out the registration form in person outside the Council Chamber. Persons who have not registered will be given an opportunity to speak following the oral testimonies of the registered speakers. 

 Remote and in-person oral testimony will be allowed when each agenda item is taken up, in the following order: 

 1.  Remote testimony; 

 2.  In-person testimony in the Council Chamber.

Requests made as early as possible have a greater likelihood of being fulfilled.

 The meeting will be viewable: (1) by internet live streaming through https://www.honolulucitycouncil.org/meetings ; (2) by televised live broadcast on ‘Ōlelo TV Channel 54; and (3) on the monitor situated outside the Council Chamber. Viewers who experience a loss of viewing signal should try switching to another viewing option.

 FOR ACTION 

 1.  BILL 8 (2023), CD1 – RELATING TO AFFORDABLE HOUSING. Promoting the development of affordable housing in the City and County of Honolulu. This ordinance does so by delaying the automatic repeal of Ordinance 19-8 [formerly Bill 7 (2019), CD2, FD1] and adding a date by which the Director of Planning and Permitting is directed to provide an updated report to the City Council with recommendations on whether the program established by Ordinance 19-8 should be repealed, modified, or extended. The new chapter ("Affordable Rental Housing") enacted by Ordinance 19-8, SECTION 2, has been codified as Chapter 32, Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 2021 and is scheduled to "sunset" on May 21, 2024. (Bill passed Second Reading and Public Hearing held on 4/19/23)

 PROPOSED CD1 TO BILL 54 (2022) (Submitted by Councilmember Say) – The CD1 (OCS2023-0382/4/27/2023 2:42 PM) makes the following amendments: 

 A.  Updates the bill to reflect that the bill is amending the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu ("ROH") 2021 (instead of the ROH 1990, as amended by Ordinance 22-7). 

 B.  In SECTION 4, changes the effective date of the ordinance to "upon its approval" (instead of on the same effective date as Ordinance 22-7). 

 C.  Makes miscellaneous technical and nonsubstantive amendments. 

 Page 4 

 PROPOSED CD1 TO BILL 54 (2022) (Submitted by Councilmember Tupola) – The CD1 (OCS2023-0377/4/27/2023 2:47 PM) makes the following amendments: 

 A.  In SECTION 2 of the bill: 

 1.  Amends the amendatory language to reference the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 2021 (instead of 1990). 

 2.  Replaces Figure 21-5.2 with a new Figure 21-5.2 that expands the areas located in the A-1 Low Density Apartment Zoning District ("A‑1 District") and the A-2 Medium Density Apartment Zoning District ("A-2 District") situated in close proximity to the Ko Olina Resort where bed and breakfast homes ("B&Bs") and transient vacation units ("TVUs") are permitted. (Current Figure 21-5.2 permits B&Bs and TVUs on various zoning lots, including only portions of zoning lots within the same zoning district – the amended Figure 21‑5.2 would include all zoning lots within a contiguous A‑1 District or A‑2 District.) 

 B.  In SECTION 4 of the bill, clarifies that the ordinance takes effect retroactive to the effective date of SECTION 10 of Ordinance 22-7 (which for ROH § 21-5.730 was October 23, 2022). 

 C.  Makes miscellaneous technical and nonsubstantive amendments.
