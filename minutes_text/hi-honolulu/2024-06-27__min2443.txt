 Testimony on BILL024(24) for Apr 17, 2024 @ 10:00 AM COUNCIL Meeting.

 041724 Council Minutes - ADOPTED 05/15/24 

 The Revised Primary Urban Center Development Plan is comprised of community‑oriented plans intended to help guide public policy, investment, and decision-making related to development in the Primary Urban Center, extending from Kāhala to Pearl City. The Plan covers five City Council Districts (Districts IV, V, VI, VII, and VIII) and nearly 20 neighborhood boards, with a vision to: 

 •  Protect and enhance Honolulu’s natural, cultural, and scenic resources; 

 •  Create livable neighborhoods with business centers, parks, plazas, and walkable streets; 

 •  Provide in-town housing choices for people of all ages and incomes; 

 •  Make Honolulu the Pacific's leading city and travel destination; and 

 •  Create a balanced transportation system that provides excellent mobility for residents and visitors. 

 The Department of Planning and Permitting will provide a briefing on key features of the Plan, including: 

 •  A summary of the Plan with a focus on community-focused design and character, equitable development, market conditions, climate change/environmental conditions, resource management, and ecological protection; and 

 •  Outlines of the unique features in each included District, such as in-fill development versus new growth areas, higher intensity development in Transit Oriented Development areas, and planning to address sea-level rise concerns in shoreline areas.
