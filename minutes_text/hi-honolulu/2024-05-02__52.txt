 FOR ACTION 

 1.  BILL 23 (2024) – RELATING TO GIFTS. Relating to gifts to City Officers and employees. (Bill passed First Reading on 4/17/24) 

 REPORTED OUT FOR 2ND READING AND SCHEDULED FOR PUBLIC HEARING

 REMOTE AND IN-PERSON TESTIFIERS 

 VOTE FOR CD1 

 (ADDENDUM) PROPOSED CD1 TO BILL 23 (2024) Submitted by Councilmember Kia’aina) - The Proposed CD1 (OCS2024-0427/4/24/2024 11:03 AM) makes the following amendments: 

 FURTHER AMENDED AND REPORTED OUT FOR 2ND READING AND SCHEDULED FOR PUBLIC HEARING 

 A. In SECTION 2 of the bill:

 1. Amends the definition of "Financial Relationship with the City" in ROH Section 1-19.7(a) by amending the second sentence of the definition to read as follows:

 "The term includes lobbying, but does not include the employer and employee relationship of a city officer or employee with the city, nor does it include financial transactions with the city such as the receipt of a refund or rebate or a similar minimal contact with the city." 

 2. Amends ROH Section 1-19.7(c) to read as follows:

 "A city officer or employee may not solicit, and, unless exempted by subsection (d), a city officer or employee may not accept or receive, either directly or indirectly through a third party, any gift if the city officer or employee knows or has reason to know that it is from a prohibited source. A city officer or employee is deemed to have reason to know that a gift is from a prohibited source if the gift is given, directly or indirectly, from any person who has appeared before the city officer or employee, or a body of which the city officer or employee is a member, in connection with the prohibited source’s lobbying activities, financial relationship with the city, or both." 

 (Makes a conforming amendment in subsection (c) to reference "prohibited source’s lobbying activities" rather than "person’s lobbying activities.") 

 3. Amends ROH Section 1-19.7(d) to delete the requirement that the gifts falling within exemptions (1), (2), and (3) be "unsolicited," and to broaden the list of donors whose inter vivos or testamentary trust distributions are exempt from the prohibitions of ROH Section 1-19.7(c). The donors added to the list in ROH Section 1-19.7(d)(2) are the City officer’s or employee’s fiancé, fiancée, relative within four degrees of consanguinity, and the spouse, domestic partner, civil union partner, fiancé, or fiancée of such a relative. The donors added to the list in ROH Section 1-19.7(d)(3) are the ancestors of the City officer or employee.

 B. Makes miscellaneous technical and nonsubstantive amendments.
