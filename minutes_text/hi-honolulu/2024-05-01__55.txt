 3.  RESOLUTION 24-92 – CHARTER AMENDMENT RELATING TO THE CREATION OF AN OCEAN SAFETY COMMISSION. Initiating amendments to the Revised Charter of the City and County of Honolulu 1973 (Amended 2017 Edition), as amended, relating to the creation of an Ocean Safety Commission. (Resolution passed First Reading on 4/17/24) 

 REPORTED OUT FOR 2ND READING AND SCHEDULED FOR PUBLIC HEARING

 REMOTE AND IN-PERSON TESTIFIERS 

 VOTE 

 PROPOSED CD1 TO RESOLUTION 24-92 (Submitted by Councilmember Weyer) - The CD1 (OCS2024-0409/4/23/2024 2:47 PM) makes the following amendments: 

 A. In Section 1, amends the question to be placed on the 2024 general election ballot to read:

 "Shall the Revised City Charter be amended to create an Ocean Safety Commission to review and make recommendations on certain activities of the Department of Ocean Safety and appoint its Director?" 

 B. In the BE IT RESOLVED clause:

 1. Amends Section 6-_01 of the Revised Charter of the City and County of Honolulu 1973 (Amended 2017 Edition), as amended, as set forth in Resolution 24-103, by providing that the Director of Ocean Safety shall be appointed by the Ocean Safety Commission, instead of the Mayor, and setting forth procedures for removing the Director.

 2. Amends Section 4-104.1 of the Revised Charter of the City and County of Honolulu 1973 (Amended 2017 Edition), as amended as set forth in Resolution 24-103, by removing the Director of Ocean Safety from the list of department heads that are nominated, appointed, and may be removed by the Mayor.

 3. Replaces all references to the “Ocean Safety Chief” with “Director of Ocean Safety.”

 C. Makes miscellaneous technical and nonsubstantive amendments.

 4.  RESOLUTION 24-81 - PROVIDE 24/7 SERVICE AT WAI’ANAE CONVENIENCE CENTER AND INCREASED MAINTENANCE AT LEEWARD O’AHU PARKS. Urging the Department of Environmental Services to provide 24/7 operational service at the Wai’anae Convenience Center and urging the Department of Parks and Recreation to provide increased maintenance at Leeward O’ahu parks and recreational facilities.
