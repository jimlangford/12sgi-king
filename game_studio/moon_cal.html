How to read the nights as game turns

Stand · Testify Kū nights (3–6) and peak light (13, 15, 20) — Ao action. Play a card, show up, voice the pono.
Listen · Hold ʻOle nights (7–10, 21–23) — Pō balancing. Listen to the record; do not force the hand tonight.
Full Light Māhealani (pō 16) — everything is visible. A major civic moment; testify in the open.
Sacred Akua, Kāne, Lono, Kāloakūkahi — bring reverence, not contention. Ceremony over debate.
Flow Medicine nights (Lāʻau), Huna, Mōhalu — let things unfold. Frame testimony to heal, not wound.
13-moon year · Sage zone mapping

Each of the 13 lunar months of the Hawaiian year is associated with a Sage zone (Mauka · Kula · Makai · Universal). The highlighted moon is the current lunation. Schematic: confirm with a kumu for the observed reckoning.

Kaulana mahina — computed from mean lunation (±1 night vs observed). The true reckoning is observed by the kumu. Civic offerings are framed as suggestions, never directives. Sacred pō bindings offered with humility; not attested without cultural review. Moon-of-year mapping is schematic. 12sgi · Kilo Aupuni · aloha · pono.
