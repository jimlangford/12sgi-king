Konane rules (5/5 verified)

Setup: Full 8×8 board alternates black and white stones. Black removes one stone adjacent to center; white removes one stone adjacent to the removed black stone.

Move: Jump one of your own stones over an adjacent opponent stone into an empty space beyond. The jumped stone is captured. Multiple captures in one move are allowed (one direction per turn).

No passing. A player who cannot capture on their turn loses.

Papamū = the board. ʻIliʻili = the smooth pebble stones. Played by the aliʻi of Hawaiʻi.
