Request the Record — and send it back to build govOS

Some of the most important data isn’t online yet — contractor names on the Lahaina rebuild permits, the FEMA housing rates, the disaster-housing contracts. But it is public record, and you have the right to request it. Below are ready-to-file requests — copy one, send it to the agency, and when the records come back, send them here so they join the public map. Many hands, one ledger.

1 · Lahaina & Kula rebuild permits — contractor & architect names

File with: County of Maui — Office of the County Clerk / Dept. of Public Works (UIPA, HRS §92F). Portal: mauicounty.gov · oversight: oip.hawaii.gov

2 · State disaster-housing contracts & rates

File with: Hawaiʻi Emergency Management Agency / Dept. of Human Services (UIPA, HRS §92F)

3 · FEMA disaster-housing rates & contractors (DR-4724-HI)

File with: FEMA FOIA Office — FEMA-FOIA@fema.dhs.gov (Freedom of Information Act, 5 U.S.C. §552)

Aloha. The record belongs to the people; sometimes it just has to be asked for. When you request a record and send it back, you turn a “pending” into a fact on the public ledger — and a pair that no longer answers gets its answer. Mahalo for carrying a stone.
